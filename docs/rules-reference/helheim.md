# Helheim: Realm of the Dead

Helheim stretches beneath a sky choked with fog, its cold, unmoving expanse slowing time itself. The air is thick with a mist made from restless souls, pale green threads that wind through the land like unspoken whispers. It clings to everything, a shroud wrapping the world in muffled silence. Tombs and graves litter the rocky landscape, crumbling into dust, as though the very stone longs to disappear along with the souls buried within.

The air is heavy, pressed down by the sorrow of countless lost souls, their presence etched into the ground. The dead are hollow-eyed shadows, wandering aimlessly, their whispers barely more than a breeze against the stone. Helheim is drained of colour, its only light the faint glow of the sickly green mist. Jagged cliffs fall into endless darkness, as if the realm has no bottom, no end—just a cold, empty void.

Yet, Helheim is not peaceful. The mist shifts with a cold purpose, moving through cracks in the stone, around graves, creeping like death itself. It is not a sudden blow, but a slow, suffocating force that seeps into the bones, draining warmth and hope with each step.

## Weather (d6)

1. **Thick Fog**: The fog thickens, reducing visibility to mere feet. All Swift Tests are made with disadvantage, and combat becomes difficult, with all attacks suffering a +2DR penalty to hit.
2. **Freezing Rain**: A freezing rain begins to fall, chilling the air but leaving a strange clarity in its wake.
3. **Silent Night**: The air falls unnaturally silent, muting all sound. Communication is impossible.
4. **Soulstorm**: A soulstorm sweeps across the land, filling the air with the wails of the lost. The party must Test Wits DR14 to maintain their composure, or forget who they are for d4 hours.
5. **Grave Mist**: A grave mist rolls in, carrying faint whispers that seem to guide the party. Those who Test Wits DR12 may gain insight into their surroundings or uncover a hidden location.
6. **Eternal Twilight**: The light dims to a faint, sickly green, making everything feel distant and unreal.

## Discoveries (d6)

1. **Forgotten Tomb**: Half-buried in the cold earth, the entrance to this forgotten tomb beckons with a sinister promise. Inside, the remains of an ancient hero lie undisturbed, clutching a Rune.
2. **Corpse Light Trail**: A trail of flickering Corpse Lights leads off into the mist. The lights dance enticingly ahead, promising a hidden crypt filled with treasure, but they may also guide the party into an ambush. Spirit Bound Guardians lie in wait, ready to strike when the party least expects it.
3. **Collector's Lair**: The party stumbles into the lair of a Bone Collector, surrounded by the twisted remains of countless dead. Among the bones, a cursed item lies waiting. But taking it will anger the Bone Collector, who will either force the party into a desperate battle or chase them down if they flee.
4. **Shattered Gravestone**: At the base of a cliff lies a shattered gravestone, its surface covered in ancient runes. Studying the stone could reveal lost knowledge. But the area is haunted by Môgudr's Sentinels, who will not hesitate to strike anyone who dares disturb the sacred ground.
5. **Frozen Pool of Souls**: A frozen pool reflects the faces of the long dead, their eyes hollow with the weight of forgotten lives. Staring into the pool allows the party to communicate with a lost soul, who may offer valuable insights or warnings. But those who gaze too long risk being pulled into the pool, their minds shattered as the soul attempts to drag them into the afterlife.
6. **Hel's Throne**: The party comes across a throne of ice and bone, said to belong to Hel herself. Sitting upon the throne grants the sitter visions of the future—glimpses of Ragnarök that may change everything. But this act draws the attention of Garmr, the spectral hound of Helheim, who will stop at nothing to claim those who sit on the throne as his own.

## Rumours (d6)

1. "Hel is building an army." Whispers say that Hel, the goddess of the underworld, is preparing for war. She's been seen picking and choosing the souls she deems worthy to serve her and her sister. Some even claim she's begun preparing for the final battle of Ragnarok, collecting the souls who will fight for her.
2. "Garmr prowls the gates." Garmr, the monstrous hound of Helheim, is said to be more restless lately. His howls echo across the land, a grim reminder of his ever-present watch over Hel's realm. Some believe he's growing restless in anticipation of Ragnarok, preparing to defend the gates of Helheim with a savagery unmatched by any mortal or god.
3. "The Nair are restless." The Nair, Helheim's Corpse Lords, are stirring in their tombs. Dark magic churns beneath their decaying flesh, growing stronger by the day. Those foolish enough to venture near their lairs often never return, their bodies claimed by the eldritch powers that these cursed beings command. The realm's balance may be on the brink of shifting, and with it, a storm of death and power.
4. "The dead are disappearing." A strange phenomenon plagues Helheim—the dead are vanishing. Souls, once bound to the cold, endless expanse of the underworld, are simply being devoured by some unknown force. Those who venture to investigate this growing mystery risk joining them, dragged into an abyss where even the dead lose their place.
5. "The Gates of Nastrond are weakening." The cursed shores of Nastrond, where the most wretched souls are eternally punished, are said to be slowly decaying. The barriers that keep the damned spirits from breaking free are fraying at the edges. If the gates fall, an unstoppable tide of vengeful souls will sweep through Helheim, and the underworld may collapse into chaos.
6. "Helheim is shrinking." The dead speak in hushed tones of Helheim's decay. The realm is slowly shrinking, the fog that once stretched endlessly now closing in around the trapped souls. Some believe the realm itself is dying, and when it fades completely, the souls held within it will be lost forever, vanishing into the void like forgotten memories.

## Encounters (d8)

1. **Modgudr's Sentinels**: d4 Modgudr's Sentinels drift through the fog, their ghostly forms barely perceptible. They attack without warning, their ethereal weapons striking with deadly precision.
2. **Bone Collectors**: Scavenging through the mist, d4 Bone Collectors are busy piecing together the remains of the fallen, creating skeletal constructs that lurk in the shadows. The party must Test Guile DR12 to spot them before the constructs spring into action. Each construct gets a free attack before battle.
3. **Corpse Lights**: Flickering in the distance, d6 Corpse Lights lure the party deeper into Helheim's depths. If they follow, the lights lead them directly into an ambush. Test Wits DR12 to see through the illusion before the battle begins.
4. **Spectral Warriors**: d2 Spirit Bound Guardians manifest from the mist, wielding ghostly weapons that strike both body and soul.
5. **Frozen Cairn**: The party stumbles across a frozen cairn, a burial site of an ancient warrior. Disturbing the cairn may awaken a Nair, one of Helheim's Corpse Lords. Test Guile DR12 to search carefully without disturbing the resting warrior, or prepare for a dangerous confrontation.
6. **Lost Souls**: A group of lost souls wanders aimlessly through the mist, their voices whispering forgotten truths. If the party lingers too long, they must Test Fortitude DR14 or succumb to despair, losing HP equal to the amount of tales in their Saga, as the souls drain their will to live.
7. **Garmr's Howl**: The haunting howl of Garmr, Helheim's spectral hound, echoes across the desolate landscape. The party must Test Fortitude DR14 to resist the paralysis of fear, or be compelled to leave Helheim as the terrifying beast draws near.
8. **Hel's Gaze**: The cold, distant gaze of Hel herself weighs upon the party. Each member must Test Fortitude DR12 to resist her icy presence. Failure results in the loss of one random tale from their Saga.

## Creatures of Helheim

### Modgudr's Sentinels
- **HP:** 6
- **Armour:** -
- **Weapon:** Spectral blade d6
- **Special:** Spectral Attack. Spectral Blade bypasses all armour. Incorporeal Form. Reduces all physical damage by half.

### Bone Collectors
- **HP:** 10
- **Armour:** -
- **Weapon:** Skeletal grip d4
- **Special:** Might Drain. A successful touch attack reduces the target's Might by 1 for the duration of the encounter.

### Spirit Bound Guardians
- **HP:** 13
- **Armour:** Ghostly armour - d2
- **Weapon:** Ethereal blade d6 (+2 damage to targets already wounded)
- **Special:** Eternal Binding. If a target falls to a Spirit Bound Guardian, their soul is immediately trapped in Helheim, making ascension to Valhalla impossible. Past Lives. Spirit Bound Guardians take the forms of former fallen party members whose names are forgotten.

### Corpse Lights
- **HP:** 6
- **Armour:** -
- **Weapon:** Flame grasp d6 fire damage (Tests Fortitude DR10 or take d4 fire damage at the start of the next round)
- **Special:** Luring Flame. Targets that can see the flame must Test Guile DR12 or be compelled to move toward the Corpse Light on their next turn, ignoring potential hazards.

### Garmr, the Guardian of Helheim
- **HP:** 30
- **Armour:** Necrotic fur - d3
- **Weapon:** Jaws of the abyss d8+3
- **Special:** Deadly Bite. On a hit, Jaws of the Abyss ignores 1 point of armour and induces a sense of dread, making the next Fortitude Test against Garmr +2 DR. Spectral Howl. Once per battle, Garmr unleashes a bonechilling howl that forces all within hearing range to Test Fortitude DR14. Failure means you are stunned from fear for d2 rounds as memories of death flood your mind. Eternal Pursuit. Garmr can teleport to any location within sight when a target attempts to flee, appearing as a dark blur that shadows their every move. This ability activates automatically if someone moves away from him without attacking.
- **Reward:** Garmr's Duty. Activate once to have Fenrir believe you are an ally for d4 rounds.

### Nair, the Corpse Lords
- **HP:** 26
- **Armour:** Amalgamation of corpses - d3
- **Weapon:** Necrotic touch d6+3
- **Special:** Aura of Decay. All close to Nair must Test Might DR12 each round or lose 2HP as the cold and decay saps their strength. Any creature that dies within their aura becomes an animated corpse under the Nair's control for the rest of the encounter. Grasp of Envy. Once per round, the Nair can extend a hand toward an enemy, forcing them to Test Wits DR14. Failure results in the target losing an item of value (weapon, Trinkets, or Rune), which disintegrates into dust.
- **Reward:** Nair's Servitude. Gain a Boney Corpse Follower (d10HP, can wield weapons, Tests made with no bonuses). If it is defeated, it can be resurrected by striking a tale from your Saga.
