# Midgard: Realm of Men

Midgard, once alive with forests, mountains, and thriving villages, is now suffocating under the relentless grip of Fimbulwinter. The sky is a constant sickly grey, barely letting in any light, while snowdrifts swallow pathways and homes. What few settlements remain cling to crumbling hearths, their warmth fleeting as food and hope grow scarce. The seas are frozen, rivers sluggish, and the wind's bitter howl cuts through the silence of a land on the brink of death.

But it's not just the cold that haunts Midgard. Draugr, undead warriors, claw their way out of frozen graves, and Trollkin descend from the mountains, their steps shaking the earth. Starving Wargr, gaunt and feral, roam the snow-covered roads. With the gods distracted by Ragnarok, the barriers that once held these horrors in check have crumbled. Monsters now roam freely, attacking the last human settlements already ravaged by frost and fear.

Survivors face a relentless battle against both the cold and the beasts that now rule the land. As the days grow darker and the frost deepens, they ponder how much longer they can hold out before Midgard succumbs to the inevitable.

## Weather (d6)

1. **Blinding Snowstorm**: A blizzard howls to life, the snow thickening into an impenetrable white wall. Visibility drops to almost nothing, and every step becomes a struggle. Navigation falters, and the icy wind bites through even the warmest gear.
2. **Icy Winds**: Bitter, knife-like winds cut through the land, causing extreme cold exposure. Unless wrapped in warm gear, Test Fortitude DR12 every 2 hours or take d4 cold damage as frostbite starts to set in.
3. **Frozen Fog**: A thick, frozen fog creeps across the land, muffling sound and swallowing sight. Beyond a few paces, the world vanishes into icy white. Tests relying on perception falter and are made with disadvantage, but the shrouded stillness lends itself to stealth—Tests for stealth gain advantage in this eerie haze.
4. **Forgotten Sun**: The sky splinters, clouds parting to reveal a pale winter sun. Its light spills over the frozen landscape, offering a fleeting reprieve from the unrelenting cold of Fimbulwinter. For a moment, the world feels less cruel, and hope lingers in the short-lived warmth.
5. **Black Ice**: Beneath the snow, black ice waits like an ill-timed joke. Step wrong, and it'll take you down, hard. Test Swift DR10 or hit the ground, cold and unforgiving, with d2 damage to remind you that winter doesn't play fair.
6. **Cloud Break**: The clouds crack open just enough to show a pale blue sky, fragile and distant. For a moment, the gloom lifts, the world less heavy, like a sigh you didn't know you were holding. It won't last, but for now, it's enough.

## Discoveries (d6)

1. **Half-Frozen Corpse**: A warrior's body lies half-buried in the snow, their weapons intact but unremarkable. Clutched in their frozen hand is a Rune, faintly glowing with an unspoken purpose.
2. **Wolf-Tracks**: Fresh tracks cut through the snow, leading to a nearby den. Inside, starving Wargr likely wait, desperate and dangerous. The den might hold spoils from past kills...
3. **Hidden Shrine**: Hidden beneath snow and ice, a small shrine to a forgotten god waits in a secluded grove. Leaving an offering or sacrifice grants a small boon (+1 to Wits Tests for the rest of the day), but disrupting its balance risks summoning a vengeful foe.
4. **Ice-Encased Spoils**: Something glints beneath the ice, catching the party's eye. Its pull is undeniable. Breaking it free demands you to Test Might DR14. Success reveals an object of power—roll on the Cursed Items table to discover its true nature.
5. **Frostbitten Supplies**: A cart lies in the snow, its frostbitten supplies offering d4 dried food and water. The provisions seem tempting, but they may be spoiled. Consuming them requires you to Test Fortitude DR12 to avoid fever.
6. **Abandoned Camp**: The scattered remains of a traveller's camp lie frozen in the snow, abandoned in haste. Among the debris are d10t Trinkets and a weathered journal, its pages recounting a failed quest to find an ancient tomb.

## Rumours (d6)

1. "The Nithing walks these woods." An old woman mutters of a Nithing spirit at the town's well, its icy form drawn by the betrayal of a local leader. She swears it marks its prey, waiting for the perfect moment to strike and dish out its cold justice.
2. "The Draugr are restless. The ground itself is opening." Farmers speak in hushed tones of Draugr clawing up from frozen graves, their numbers growing with each passing night. Some say something has stirred them from their rest, and as Fimbulwinter tightens its grip, the worst is yet to come.
3. "Trollkin have stolen our food!" A haggard group of villagers pleads for help—their food stocks have been stolen by Trollkin. Desperation lines their faces as they offer what little they have left to anyone brave enough to track the creatures and reclaim their provisions.
4. "They say Odin's ravens see everything." Travellers murmur that Huginn and Muninn are more vigilant than ever, their dark shapes smudging the skies. The Allfather gathers knowledge, his gaze falling on mortals tied to Ragnarok. To catch their attention, they say, is to be marked—for greatness... or for doom.
5. "Fimbulwinter's only the start." A wandering seer, cloaked in frost and mystique, warns that Fimbulwinter is only the prelude. Their visions speak of Ragnarok's nearness but hint at something else—an ancient force lurking in Midgard's mountains, more terrifying than even the Jotnar.

## Encounters (d8)

1. **Nithing's Judgement**: A Nithing appears, a twisted spirit born from broken oaths. It seeks vengeance on—yours or another's. To stop it, you must confront the wrong, make amends, or be dragged to a place where regret never fades.
2. **Frost-Stalker Bjorn**: Bjorn, a once-mighty warrior now lost to the endless winter, lurks in the obscurity of the trees. His eyes burn with hunger, his movements predatory. Draped in beast furs, he strikes with the raw ferocity of a cornered animal. Confronting him is perilous—his knowledge of the woods gives him an edge, and his savagery is unmatched. Defeat him, and you may claim his Trinkets, but beware: Bjorn is no ordinary foe.
3. **Wargr Ambush**: In the moonlit snow, a pack of d4 Wargr stalk, their glowing eyes piercing through the darkness. Their howls echo long before they strike, the hors d'oeuvre to the hunt. When they attack, it's with deadly precision, moving as one, relentless and calculating.
4. **Trollkin Mischief**: The village lies in disarray—walls shattered, food stores raided, livestock scattered. The Trollkin cause mischief everywhere, leaving the villagers desperate but too terrified to act. The party can track the miscreants into the wilds, but these creatures won't go down easily. d3 Trollkin lay traps and turn the terrain itself into a weapon.
5. **Draugr Raid**: From the snow, a band of Draugr (equal to the number of PCs) shambles forth, their frozen armour cracking with each step, their eyes glowing with eternal rage. They attack without hesitation, driven by an unending thirst for battle. Slaying them offers rewards—ancient weapons from the old world, some enchanted, others cursed...
6. **Starving Villagers**: A huddled group of starving villagers stumbles into view, their stomachs growling with hunger. They beg for food, offering scraps of belongings in trade, desperation etched into every word. But desperation is a dangerous thing—if they feel threatened or denied, their pleas may give way to violence, driven by the instinct to survive.
7. **Mara's Nightmare**: A Mara has latched onto one of the party, a sinister spirit feeding on their dreams. Each night, it twists their sleep into a labyrinth of terror, leaving them fatigued and drained (d4 damage). Without intervention, the Mara will return night after night, sapping their life and sanity until nothing remains.
8. **Frozen Ruins**: The party discovers the frozen remains of an ancient village. Among the crumbled ruins, d3 Runes carved into stones hint at a hidden path to a lost treasure or divine artefact. But the air feels thick with warning—disturbing the site risks waking the d4 Draugr who guard it...

## Creatures of Midgard

### Trollkin
- **HP:** 10
- **Armour:** -
- **Weapon:** Stolen tools d6

### Wargr
- **HP:** 8
- **Armour:** Thick fur - d2
- **Weapon:** Claw d4, Bite d6

### Draugr
- **HP:** 6
- **Armour:** Battle worn armour - d2
- **Weapon:** Varied weapons. Roll d6: 1-2=Sling d4 ranged, 3-4=Sword d6, 5-6=Spear d8

### Mara
- **HP:** 5
- **Armour:** -
- **Weapon:** Soul drain d8
- **Special:** Fear Factor. Will always attack the most fearful. Any damage dealt to the Mara is also dealt to the attacker.

### Frost-Stalker Bjorn
- **HP:** 22
- **Armour:** Bear hide - d2
- **Weapon:** Bear hands d8+2
- **Special:** Stalker's Prey. If the characters are within a mile of Bjorn, he knows where they are.
- **Reward:** Blessing of the Frost Stalker. Pick a prey (enemy type or named foe) if you are within a mile of your prey, you know exactly where they are.

### The Nithing
- **HP:** 15
- **Armour:** Ghostly robes - d2
- **Weapon:** Withered fingers d6+2
- **Special:** Liar's Remorse. Whomever goes back on their word in Midgard, the Nithing will find and punish them.
- **Reward:** The Nithing's Intuition. You automatically know when someone is lying and are compelled to enact justice.
