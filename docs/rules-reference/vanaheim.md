# Vanaheim: Realm of the Vanir

Vanaheim, once a land of gentle breezes and harmony, has been ravaged by Fimbulwinter's savage winds. Tornadoes tear across the plains, uprooting trees and flattening villages, while storms batter the forests relentlessly. The balance the Vanir gods once maintained has been swept away, leaving the realm in chaos.

The mighty trees of Vanaheim, once symbols of growth and fertility, now lie broken, and the rivers, once calm, have turned into wild torrents filled with debris. The people, once at peace with nature, now huddle in shelters, praying for protection from the gales that never cease.

Even the Vanir gods are not immune to the devastation. Freyr's once-bountiful fields are now barren, swept away by storms, and Njord, god of the seas and winds, has lost control of the elements he once commanded. The creatures of Vanaheim have turned wild, their minds broken by the endless howling winds.

## Weather (d6)

1. **Tornado Uprising**: Massive tornadoes tear through the plains, uprooting trees and shredding the landscape. The party must Test Swift DR14 or be caught in the winds, suffering d6 damage and losing any sense of direction.
2. **Elemental Storm**: A wild elemental storm roars across the land. The sky is filled with lightning, ice shards, and gale-force winds.
3. **Howling Gale**: The winds howl relentlessly, carrying faint songs through the air. Though disorienting, those who listen carefully may glean fragmented wisdom from the Vanir's ancient knowledge. Test Wits DR12 to discern a useful clue or insight.
4. **Flooded Plains**: The rivers have overflowed, turning the once-fertile plains into a dangerous flood zone. Movement is restricted, and anyone attempting to cross the waters must Test Might DR12 to avoid being swept away.
5. **Blinding Rain**: Sheets of cold rain pour from the sky, obscuring vision and making the ground treacherous. The party suffers –2 to all ranged attacks and must Test Swift DR12 to avoid slipping on the soaked terrain.
6. **Veil of Frost**: A gentle frost settles over the land, painting the trees and plains in shimmering silver. The cold is brisk but not harmful, and its beauty seems almost calming.

## Discoveries (d6)

1. **Overgrown Shrine**: The party discovers an overgrown shrine to an ancient Vanir god hidden beneath vines and debris. Leaving a sacrifice of food or water at the altar grants a blessing, bestowing +1 to Swift Tests for the rest of the day.
2. **Touched Tree**: A tree, split by lightning, reveals a hidden cache of d6 Runes within its hollow trunk.
3. **Vanir Relic**: Buried in the mud, the party uncovers a cursed Vanir relic—a weapon or item humming with ancient nature magic. Roll on the cursed item table.
4. **Gullveig's Altar**: The party discovers a hidden altar to Gullveig, the enchantress, adorned with gold and Trinkets left by fearful supplicants. Taking these offerings invites her wrath, summoning Gullveig to exact vengeance and reclaim what is hers.
5. **Flooded Cave**: A half-submerged cave entrance looms ahead, its dark depths beckoning the party. Inside, remnants of Vanir Trinkets glimmer in the dim light, promising rewards. However, the cave is dangerously unstable—at any moment, it may flood. Escaping in time requires you to Test Swift DR12, or the party risks being trapped as the waters rise.
6. **Seed of Freyr**: In the centre of a ruined field, the party discovers a single glowing seed of Freyr, pulsing with vibrant life. If planted, it instantly sprouts into a small grove, providing shelter and restoring an additional d6HP to those who rest within. However, removing the seed from Vanaheim risks invoking a wrath.

## Rumours (d6)

1. "Freyr is missing." The god of peace, who made the earth sing, vanished without a trace. Some say an Aesir god dragged him off, others claim he just walked away, tired of carrying a world that's rotting from the roots.
2. "Njord has lost control." The seas don't listen anymore. Njord, god of the waves, is just another old man shouting at clouds that don't care. The winds tear through like wild dogs, and people say Gullveig cursed him, laughing at his struggle while the waters drown the land.
3. "Gullveig has returned." She's back, Gullveig, the one who smiles while the world burns. Her power wraps around the chaos, tightening like a noose. They say she's not just bending nature; she's pulling it apart, weaving it into something only she can control. And if the gods stand in her way? She'll topple them like broken idols.
4. "The Vanir have abandoned us." Prayers are dead letters now. The Vanir aren't answering—maybe they're gone, maybe they just don't care anymore. The land is cold, the spirits are angry, and the people are left to freeze while the gods turn their backs.
5. "The Grove Wardens have awoken." The Grove Wardens are moving, slow and heavy, like the forest itself got tired of being cut down. Some say they're reclaiming what's theirs; others think they're gearing up for a fight no one's ready for. Either way, they don't care about who's innocent and who's guilty.
6. "A power in the wind." The wind doesn't just howl—it calls, whispers promises of power to anyone stupid enough to listen. Some try to grab it, thinking they can bend the storm. Most of them don't make it. The ones who do? They're never quite right again.

## Encounters (d8)

1. **Vanir Guardians**: d4 Vanir Guardians, clad in leaf-encrusted armour, peer silently from the treetops, their presence steeped in ancient power. If angered or attacked, they summon the wrath of the land—roots snare, trees strike, and the party must endure the forest's living fury.
2. **Skirnir the Corrupted**: The party is confronted by Skirnir, Freyr's former messenger, now twisted by dark forces. His eyes shine with madness as he commands the winds and rivers to lash out, his voice seething with reprisal against the Vanir gods for forsaking him. You could Test Wits DR16 to try and calm his fury and spare Vanaheim from destruction, but failure means dealing with him yourself.
3. **Landvaettir Ambush**: 2 Landvaettir emerge from the earth, their forms woven from the terrain they inhabit, towering and watchful. They accuse the party of trespassing, their voices heavy with the weight of the land.
4. **Grove Wardens**: As the party nears, d3 Grove Wardens stir from the forest, their massive, sentient forms nearly indistinguishable from the surrounding trees. They observe in silence, measuring the party's intentions. Any harm to the forest's trees or animals will awaken their fury, triggering a relentless assault as the wardens call the forest itself to join the fight.
5. **Gullveig's Influence**: The oppressive presence of Gullveig, the ancient witch, weighs heavily on the realm. Her enchantments twist reality, unleashing arcane storms that crackle with malevolent energy. Anyone caught in the storm must Test Fortitude DR14 to evade the deadly heave.
6. **River Nymphs**: Beneath the churning surface of a storm-tossed river, d6 River Nymphs watch the party with eyes like liquid silver. Their songs, haunting and sweet, promise safe passage, but lingering too long forces you to Test Guile DR14 to resist their pull.
7. **Fimbulwinter's Gale**: The storm's winds howl with an unnatural rage, knocking the party off balance and biting with ferocity. Anyone exposed must Test Fortitude DR14 or be blown away.
8. **Njord's Fury**: As the party nears a coast, they face the raging seas of Njord, now wild and untamed. Towering waves crash with unnatural fury, threatening to engulf all in their path. Navigating the shoreline demands that you Test Might DR14 to avoid being swept away.

## Creatures of Vanaheim

### Vanir Guardians
- **HP:** 6
- **Armour:** Leaf-encrusted armour - d2
- **Weapon:** Twisted bow d6 ranged
- **Special:** Veil of Leaves. Once per battle, a Vanir Guardian can shroud itself or an ally in a camouflage of leaves and vines, making them invisible for one round unless they attack.

### Landvaettir
- **HP:** 8
- **Armour:** (Unless on rocky terrain) - d2
- **Weapon:** Weapons are based on the terrain: Rocky = Boulders d8 ranged; Forested = Poison Mist (Test Fortitude DR12 or become poisoned); Water/Wet = Crashing waves d6; All Terrain = Slam d4
- **Special:** Form of the Land. The Landvaettir changes based on its terrain. Rocky = Armour - d4; Forested = Can entangle an enemy, Test Fortitude DR12 or be stunned for one round; Water/Wet = DR to hit +2.

### River Nymphs
- **HP:** 5
- **Armour:** -
- **Weapon:** Screech d4
- **Special:** Siren Song. Once per encounter, a River Nymph can sing an enchanting melody. Enemies who can hear the song must Test Guile DR14 or be compelled to move toward the Nymph on their next turn.

### Grove Wardens
- **HP:** 6
- **Armour:** Thick bark - d3
- **Weapon:** Vine whip d6
- **Special:** Regeneration Flame. Regains 1HP at the start of each round unless damaged by fire. Fire also deals double damage to the Wardens.

### Gullveig the Enchantress
- **HP:** 24
- **Armour:** Nature's ward - d2
- **Weapon:** Vinewoven staff d4
- **Special:** Resistant to Fire. Gullveig takes only half damage from fire-based attacks. Witchcraft Arsenal: Roll d4 each round – 1=Summon Boar (d6 damage); 2=Hexfire Blast (all targets Test Swift DR12 or take d8 damage); 3=Vine Bind (Test Might DR14 or be stunned); 4=Curse of Weakness (target Test Guile DR12 or take -2 to all Tests next turn).
- **Reward:** Enchantress' Hunger. Activate once to deal double damage to Aesir Gods for d4 rounds.

### Skirnir the Corrupted
- **HP:** 10
- **Armour:** Tattered leather robes - d2
- **Weapon:** Redwood bastard sword d10+1
- **Special:** Don't Shoot the Messenger. Once per encounter, he can target one character and attempt to charm them. The target must Test Guile DR14 or become temporarily enthralled by Skirnir's will, turning against their allies and attacking the nearest party member for the next round. This effect ends after one round or if the enthralled character takes damage.
- **Reward:** Corrupted Negotiator. One use; anyone will agree to a bargain or trade of your choosing, no matter how outlandish. They will, however, be aware of the trick after d12 hours.
