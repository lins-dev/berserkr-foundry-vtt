# Classes

## 1 Midgard's Berserkr
Berserkrs, hailing from the wild and untamed lands of Midgard, are legendary warriors feared across the Nine Realms for their unmatched ferocity in battle. They fight with the strength of the gods and the fury of wild beasts, driven by an inner rage that ignites in the heat of combat.

Berserkrs are known to charge headfirst into the thick of battle, their war cries echoing across the battlefield, shrugging off wounds that would fell lesser men.

**Starting Abilities:** Roll 3d6.

**REALM SPECIAL:** Wrath of Man. If you deliver the final blow to an enemy you heal 1HP.

**Starting Equipment:** Heavy War-Axe (d8 damage). d4 food, d4 water. 2d6×10‡ Trinkets.

**Wyrd of the Wolf-Skin:** Once per day, you can enter a trance that blurs the line between man and beast, lasting for d6 rounds. In this state, you are granted a +2 to attacks and can track enemies by scent. When the trance ends, Test Wits DR14. On a failure, the lingering spirit of the wolf compels you to lash out at the nearest being, driven by a hunger beyond conscious discernment.

**Iron Vigor:** The iron within your blood fortifies your will, mingling with the essence of your fallen foes. When you drink the blood of a defeated enemy, taking a full round to do so, you recover d6HP and do double damage on your next attack. But blood is a double-edged sword—if your enemy's blood was tainted, Test Fortitude DR12. On a failure, you are afflicted with a raging fever.

**Storm of the Aesir:** Your rage summons the fury of the storm, a tempest that crackles around your weapon. Once per battle, after a successful attack, you can call upon this power, causing your attack to deal an additional d6 lightning damage.

**Oath to the Ancestors:** You are bound by blood to your ancestors—their eyes are ever upon you, judging your every deed. Once per session, you can call upon their aid, allowing you to reroll any failed attack or defence.

**Berserker's Toll:** Pain becomes your ally, each battle etching its mark upon your body and soul. After taking damage, you can choose to channel your suffering into raw power. For every 3HP lost, you gain a +1 bonus to damage rolls for the remainder of the battle.

**Blood Eagle Ritual:** In the most desperate of moments you invoke the ancient, dreaded Blood Eagle, a ritual that demands both time and soul. This gruesome act takes a full round and can only be performed once per session. Your next attack automatically hits, dealing maximum damage as the gods momentarily favour your brutality. But this act leaves a mark on you—Test Might DR14 at the end of the session. On a failure, the spirit of the slain haunts you, bringing nightmares and a -1 penalty to all rolls until the spirit is appeased through sacrifice or ritual.

## 2 Asgard's Valkyrie
Selected by Odin himself, the Valkyries are warrior maidens who move with the grace of a tempest and the inevitability of fate. Tasked with carrying the souls of the fallen to Valhalla, they stand as both guide and guardian, walking the razor's edge between life and death.

Trained in the sacred arts of battle and blessed with the Allfather's divine magic, the Valkyries command awe, their presence as ominous as the storm clouds that signal a coming reckoning.

**Starting Abilities:** Roll 3d6.

**REALM SPECIAL:** Flight. You can fly. Roll d6 at the end of every round. On a result of 1, you must land for d4 rounds to catch your breath.

**Starting Equipment:** A suit of Divine Armour (tier 4). Spear of the Valkyrie (d10 damage). Sword of the Valkyrie (d8 damage). d4 food, d4 water. 3d6×10‡ Trinkets.

**Eyes of the Fallen:** Once per day, you can peer into the soul of a creature, uncovering its deepest flaw, its greatest weakness—a truth it cannot hide. This revelation grants you a +2 bonus to attacks against that creature for the rest of the battle, striking with the precision of one who sees the end before it begins.

**Wings of the Storm:** Once per day, ethereal wings unfurl, allowing you to take to the skies for d6 rounds. Dive-bombing your enemies from above, your attacks gain a +2 bonus, each strike delivered with the force of a thunderclap, as if the sky itself has joined your battle.

**Voice of the Chooser:** Once per battle, you can issue a command that must be obeyed. Test Fortitude DR14. On a success, one enemy of your choice is compelled to follow your command for the next round—whether it's to stand down, turn their weapon on an ally, or flee in terror.

**Spear of the Worthy:** Once per battle, when an ally is attacked, you can interpose your spear, infused with divine protection. Test Fortitude DR14. On a success, the attack is completely negated, the force of the blow dissipating as if it had struck the unyielding will of the gods themselves.

**Raven's Favour:** Once per day, you can call upon raven spirits to scout ahead or relay information from afar. They reveal hidden dangers or secrets in the area, granting you and your allies advantage on your next Test. In their whispers, you hear the murmur of the world's hidden truths, guiding you through the darkness.

**Valhalla's Call:** Once per day, unleash a battle cry that reverberates with the spirit of fallen heroes. All allies that can hear you gain a +2 bonus to attacks for d6 rounds, their spirits lifted by the echo of Valhalla's call, as if the very halls of the afterlife are cheering them on.

## 3 Vanaheim's Vanir Warden
The Vanir Wardens are the silent guardians of the natural world, hailing from Vanaheim, where the verdant forests whisper ancient secrets. Masters of forgotten magic, their souls are deeply intertwined with the earth, flora, and fauna.

In battle, they command the strength of nature, wielding the elements as extensions of their will.

**Starting Abilities:** Roll 3d6.

**REALM SPECIAL:** Nature's Lore. When healing any amount, add an additional 1HP.

**Starting Equipment:** A robe of woven leaves and vines. Druidic Staff (d6 damage). Old pouch of Healing Herbs (d6 uses, each use heals d4HP). d4 food, d4 water. d6×10‡ Trinkets.

**Nature's Wrath:** You call upon the ancient forces lying dormant beneath the earth, coaxing roots, vines, or thorns to rise and strike your foes. Once per day, Test Wits DR12 to summon this primal force. On a success, d3 enemies you can see are ensnared for d3 rounds, the ground itself becoming their captor. They take d3 damage per round and cannot move.

**Nature's Shield:** Your skin hardens, taking on the texture and resilience of ancient trees. Once per day, you can transform your skin into bark for d6 rounds, gaining +2 to armour negation.

**Healing Waters:** You summon the sacred waters of Vanaheim, feeling the cool, life-giving liquid swirl around your hands before flowing over wounds, knitting flesh and restoring vitality. Once per day, you may heal yourself or an ally for d6+2HP. These waters cleanse both body and soul, washing away negative conditions (fever, fatigue, poison and blindness).

**Beast Companion:** A loyal beast from Vanaheim walks beside you, its presence a reminder that you're never truly alone in the wild. This creature—whether a... Roll d6: (When your companion makes a Test, roll d20 against the DR)
1-2 Wolf (6HP, Bite d6 damage)
3-4 Hawk (4HP, Talon d4 damage)
5-6 Bear (8HP, Claw d8 damage)
...fights by your side, sharing in your struggles and triumphs. In battle, the beast acts immediately after your turn. If it falls, you mourn it as part of yourself, but after a d4 days, you may summon a new companion, for the bond between you and the wild is never truly severed.

**Vanir's Blessing:** The Vanir have touched your mind with the gift of foresight, a gentle nudge toward your destiny. Once per session, you may reroll any failed Test, as the threads of fate realign in your favour. Additionally, this feature allows you to sense the direction of the nearest source of water, food, or shelter when travelling.

**Verdant Rebirth:** Once per day, you may channel the primal energy of Vanaheim to restore life to the surrounding environment. Plants sprout, bloom, and intertwine around you, creating a temporary sanctuary. The flora grants the following benefits for d6 rounds:
- Allies gain +2 to defence as the vegetation shields them.
- Any ally starting their turn within the sanctuary regains 1HP, as the land's vitality flows into them.

## 4 Jotunheim's Frost Jötunn
Frost Giants are the ancient, towering behemoths of Jotunheim, beings of immense strength and chilling power. As a Frost Giant—or Jötunn—you are the mountain unshackled. You're both feared and revered across the realms.

Your power is an icy wrath flowing through your veins like a frozen river, relentless and unyielding.

**Starting Abilities:** Roll 3d6.

**Starting Equipment:** Thick fur coat. Giant Club (d12 damage). d4 food, d4 water. d6×10 Trinkets.

**Titan's Might:** Your Might is legendary, capable of reshaping the world around you. Once per day, you can perform a feat of extraordinary power. This may involve lifting and hurling massive boulders, breaking through barriers that defy others, or delivering an additional d10 damage with a melee attack.

**Iced Aegis:** Once per session, you can summon an icy barrier that absorbs incoming harm. For the next d4 rounds, any damage you take is reduced by half as shards of frost erupt on impact to deflect the blow. Additionally, enemies striking you in melee combat during this time take 1 damage from the freezing backlash.

**Frostbound Grasp:** Your touch carries the chill of deep winter, freezing anything it contacts. Once per day, you can encase a foe or object in ice, stunning it for d2 rounds. If the target is a living creature, it takes d6 damage at the start of every round.

**Winter's Fury:** With a deep breath, you summon the full wrath of winter, exhaling a biting wind that creates a localised blizzard. Once per day, you can unleash this blizzard around you, dealing d8 damage to all enemies close to you.

**Glacial Empowerment:** The icy tundras of Jotunheim enhance your strength, grounding you in the elemental force of your homeland. When standing on snow or ice, you gain +2 to all Tests. As you move, you leave behind icy patches.

**Ymir's Revival:** Once per lifetime, when reduced to 0HP, you can call upon the ancient power of Jotunheim to encase yourself in ice. This icy shell absorbs all damage, delaying death and giving you a chance to recover. While encased, you are immobile until the ice is thawed. Only the fires of Musphelheim can melt the ice.

## 5 Alfheim's Light Elf
Hailing from the radiant realm of Alfheim, Light Elves are beings of celestial grace and ethereal power. Their very presence illuminates the darkest corners of the world, a living bridge between the mortal plane and the divine.

With an inherent connection to the light, they wield its power, not only to protect and heal, but also to reveal hidden truths and drive back the encroaching darkness.

**Starting Abilities:** Roll 3d6.

**REALM SPECIAL:** Dark Vision. You can see in the dark. Be sure to let the GM know often.

**Starting Equipment:** Flowing white robes. Lightwood Staff (d6 damage). Set of Light Grenades (d4 grenades, d6 damage, causes blindness). d4 food, d4 water. 2d6×10‡ Trinkets.

**Blinding Radiance:** Once per day, release a burst of intense light, blinding all enemies within your immediate area for d3 rounds.

**Veil of Light:** Once per session you can envelop yourself or an ally in a glowing shield for d6 rounds. This veil grants +2 to defence and heals d4HP, though simple enemies will be drawn to you like moth to a flame.

**Luminous Healing:** Once per day, heal yourself or an ally for d6+2HP. This healing light also removes negative conditions (fever, fatigue, poison and blindness).

**Sunbeam Strike:** Focus sunlight into a beam of pure energy. Once per battle, Test Guile DR12 to target a single enemy within sight and deal d8 damage, ignoring armour and directly burning through any defences.

**Radiant Form:** Once per session, transform into a being of pure light for d3 rounds, gaining +4 to defence. You can move through solid objects, barriers and enemies. If you move through an enemy this way, you deal d4 damage ignoring armour and gain some of their memories.

**Beacon of Hope:** Summon a pillar of light once per day that lasts for d6 rounds. Allies within this beacon gain +2 to attack and defence and are immune to fear and ability altering effects, their spirits bolstered by the unwavering light.

## 6 Svartalfheim's Master Smith
The Master Smiths of Svartalfheim are legendary artisans whose skill in the forge transcends mere craftsmanship. These alchemists of metal and magic are renowned not only for their creation of formidable weapons and armour but also for their unparalleled mastery of runic enchantments.

With hammers and tongs in hand, they infuse each piece with ancient runes, imbuing their creations with divine power.

**Starting Abilities:** Roll 3d6.

**REALM SPECIAL:** Smith's Will. Smiths can discern the value and original craftsman of pretty much any weapon or armour.

**Starting Equipment:** Smith's Tools. Sturdy Chainmail (tier 3). Smith's Hammer (d6 damage, can be used as a tool). Choose or roll 3 random Runes (these Runes can only be inscribed into armour/weapons). d4 food, d4 water. 4d6×10‡ Trinkets.

**Runic Mastery:** When you inscribe a Rune onto a weapon or armour, its effects are enhanced (GM discretion). For example:
1. Rune of Isa (Weapon Inscribed): "Activate to coat your weapon in frost for d4 rounds. Each strike deals an additional d4 cold damage." This could become d6 rounds and an additional d6 cold damage.
2. Rune of Berkano (Armour Inscribed): "Activate to create a healing aura. For the next d4 rounds, you and all allies close to you heal d4HP at the start of their turn." This could become d6 rounds and d6HP.

**Runic Reforging:** You can reforge an item to hold two Runes simultaneously, allowing the effects to synergise for better or worse. This process takes a long rest. When using combined effects, if a fumble is rolled, the user loses double the HP and cannot use runic magic until they take a long rest.

**Rune of Shattering:** Inscribe a weapon with the Rune of Shattering to turn it into a one-use, devastating tool. Upon striking, the weapon deals maximum damage and shatters, causing a shock wave that inflicts d6 damage to anyone nearby. Only one weapon at a time can have the Rune of Shattering.

**Smith's Resilience:** Your experience in the forge has fortified your body and spirit. Gain +2 to Fortitude Tests, and once per session, negate a defence fumble, reducing its damage to 0.

**Anvil-Bound Will:** Once per session, you can call upon the ancient bond between the Dwarves and their forges to imbue either a weapon or piece of armour with temporary strength. Test Wits DR12. For the next battle:
- A Weapon gains +2 damage and the ability to ignore armour for the next d6 attacks.
- Armour negates an additional 2 damage for the next d6 rounds as molten runes briefly flare with protective power.

**Quicksmith:** Your speed and efficiency in the forge are unmatched. Once per session, upgrade or repair a weapon or piece of armour in rapid time, completing the task in just 1 hour. You can also change one inscribed Rune for another that you have seen or interacted with before.

## 7 Niflheim's Wraith
Niflheim's Wraiths are beings forged from the cold, misty expanse of the underworld, their essence forever intertwined with the deathly realm of Niflheim. These harbingers of despair wield the eerie powers of mist and death with a quiet, unsettling grace.

As a Niflheim Wraith, you carry the chill of the afterlife with you, a constant reminder that the boundary between the living and the dead is thinner than most would dare to acknowledge.

**Starting Abilities:** Roll 3d6.

**REALM SPECIAL:** Stench of Death. You stink. Which isn't great, but you also can smell death from up to a mile away.

**Starting Equipment:** Ghostly robes. Spectral Dagger (d6 damage). d4 food, d4 water. d6×10 Trinkets.

**Misty Apparition:** You can dissolve into spectral mist, becoming nearly invisible and incorporeal, a whisper on the wind. Once per day, transform into mist for d3 rounds. In this form, you slip through solid objects, immune to physical attacks. However, magic and supernatural forces can still harm you.

**Wail of the Lost:** The despair of countless lost souls echoes through you, a chilling wail that pierces reality. Once per day, unleash this scream, causing all enemies within earshot to be gripped by fear. Affected enemies are compelled to run away from you for d3 rounds.

**Ghostly Step:** Your connection to the spectral realm allows you to move as silently and unseen as the mist. Once per day, automatically succeed on any stealth-related action regardless of difficulty.

**Soulbound Weapon:** This weapon is more than just steel—it's a vessel for a trapped spirit, its essence woven into the blade, which deals d8 damage. This soulbound weapon strikes incorporeal enemies as if they were solid and deals an additional d4 damage to the living. On a fumble, the weapon releases the enemy within...

**Mark of the Dead:** You place a deathly mark on a target, a spectral sigil binding them to the inevitable approach of death. Once per battle, mark an enemy with this sign for d6 rounds. Each round, the DR to hit the marked enemy is reduced by 1.

**Commune with the Lost:** You reach beyond the veil, touching the minds of the dead and drawing forth their whispers. Once per session, commune with a dead person, asking up to three questions. Two answers are truthful. One will always be a lie.

## 8 Muspelheim's Flame Construct
Forged in the unending fires of Muspelheim, the Flame Constructs are living embodiments of the realm's molten fury. Their smouldering stone bodies drip with magma, every movement sending ripples of heat through the air, scorching the earth beneath them.

As relentless sentinels, they leave molten scars in their wake, administering their volcanic power with terrifying precision.

**Starting Abilities:** Roll 3d6.

**REALM SPECIAL:** Fire Resistant. Flame Constructs take only half damage from fire-based attacks.

**Starting Equipment:** Searing Chain (d12 damage). Ember core (once per day, absorb heat from the surroundings to heal d6HP). d4 food, d4 water. d6×10 Trinkets.

**Immolation Aura:** Once per day, you can engulf yourself in flames for d6 rounds. Enemies that strike you in melee take d4 fire damage. The aura also ignites any flammable materials nearby.

**Molten Surge:** Once per day, you can channel the volcanic power of Muspelheim, surging forward in a burst of lava and flame. Move through enemies and obstacles, dealing d6 fire damage to all in your path.

**Pyroclastic Fist:** Unleash a devastating punch fuelled by volcanic force. Once per battle, make an attack with +3. If successful, the target takes d8 damage and is knocked prone.

**Living Flame:** Once per day, you can ignite your entire body in an inferno, doubling the damage you deal for d4 rounds. However, once this blaze dies down, you are left weakened, suffering a -2 penalty to all Tests for the rest of the day.

**Eruption Call:** Once per day, summon a localised eruption of lava from the ground beneath your enemies. This eruption lasts d3 rounds, dealing d6 damage each round to those caught within it.

**Inferno Heart:** The hotter the battle, the stronger you become. For every 5 points of fire damage your enemies take, gain +1 to all Tests until the end of battle.

## 9 Helheim's Soul Shepherd
Soul Shepherds are guides to lost souls, grim guardians who walk the shadowed paths of Helheim. Tasked by Hel herself, the Soul Shepherds move between realms, carrying out the will of the underworld. The cold touch of death lingers in every step, and the dead follow in their wake, bound to the Shepherds' wills.

Their power lies not just in their mastery over death, but in their understanding of the delicate balance between life and the afterlife.

**Starting Abilities:** Roll 3d6.

**REALM SPECIAL:** Chat With a Goner. If a person has just died you can chat with them for up to one minute.

**Starting Equipment:** Cloak of Forgotten Souls (can turn invisible in shadows once per day for d4 rounds). Death's Crook (d8 damage). d4 food, d4 water. d6×10 Trinkets.

**Requiem of Rest:** Once per day, you may sing a haunting melody that resonates with the spirits of the dead. Test Swift DR12 to lull nearby enemies into a trance-like state for d3 rounds. While entranced, enemies cannot attack or take aggressive actions unless directly harmed.

**Soul Binding:** You may bind the soul of a creature that has recently died. When a humanoid creature dies in your presence, you may attempt to bind its soul. Test Swift DR14. Once bound, the soul can be commanded to perform simple tasks—scouting or attacking with d6 damage (ignoring armour). The soul remains bound for d6 hours before fading into Helheim's mists. Only one soul may be bound at any time.

**Hel's Embrace:** Once per day, you may channel the chilling grasp of Helheim through your touch. On a successful attack, you drain d6HP from an enemy, healing yourself for the same amount.

**Veil of Death:** Once per session, you may blur the line between life and death, making an ally appear as an incorporeal ghost. For d4 rounds, enemies will ignore the veiled target, perceiving it as though it has already passed into the afterlife. While veiled, the target cannot attack or interact with the physical world but may move freely, even passing through barriers or enemies.

**The Spirit Path:** You may summon a bridge of spirits over impassable terrain, whether a chasm, river, wall or otherwise. The spectral bridge lasts for d6 hours and can support you and your companions. Those who cross must sacrifice something to the spirits—d4HP, a cherished tale from their Saga, or another form of essence.

**Hel's Bargain:** In moments of near death, you may strike a dark bargain with Hel herself. Once in your lifetime, when an ally dies, you may negotiate with the Lady of Helheim. Hel will allow the ally to return to life, but at a grave cost—they must strike all but one tale from their Saga. (This cannot be used if Hel is dead).

## 10 Realmbreaker
Realmbreakers move like a shadow through the Nine Realms, slipping between cracks in reality, bound by neither time nor place. They command runic magic, ancient symbols that hold the weight of forgotten gods, bending them to shape the threads of existence.

Every step they take alters the balance of fate, worlds shifting like ripples in still water.

**Starting Abilities:** Roll 3d6.

**REALM SPECIAL:** Someone Else's Memories. You have all the memories of all previous characters played by the PC.

**Starting Equipment:** Choose or roll 3 random Runes (these Runes can only be cast as spells). d4 food, d4 water. 3d6×10‡ Trinkets.

**Rune Storm:** You can unleash a chaotic burst of raw Rune magic. Once per session, Test Wits DR14 to create a storm of random magical effects. The storm deals d6 damage to all creatures caught in it and causes a random magical effect to occur (roll and combine two random Runes. GM's Discretion). On a failure, the storm erupts uncontrollably. (GM picks and combines two Runes).

**Rune Expertise:** You excel at casting Runes, gaining +2 to Wits Tests when casting them.

**Temporary Inscription:** Once per day, you may overcharge a Rune, casting it with amplified force. Test Wits DR14. On success, the Rune's effect is doubled (e.g., increased damage, extended duration). On failure, treat the Rune as if it had been fumbled.

**Realmstep:** You can momentarily step into another realm to avoid danger. Once per day, after being targeted by an attack, you may Test Wits DR12 to step into another realm for a brief moment, avoiding the attack entirely and reappearing in the same location.

**Walker of Worlds:** You are attuned to the cosmic forces of realm travel. All Tests for realm travel are made with +2 bonus.

**Rift Walker:** You can create small, temporary tears between realms to traverse short distances. Once per day, you may open a rift, instantly teleporting yourself and one ally to a visible location you can see.
