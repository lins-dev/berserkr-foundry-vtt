# Niflheim: Realm of Eternal Ice

Niflheim stretches out where time itself feels frozen beneath layers of frost. The cold isn't just felt—it owns you, seeping into your bones, erasing warmth in seconds. The land, vast and barren, is scarred by deep fissures, cracks in the ice like forgotten wounds. From these gashes, pale, formless spirits rise, weaving through the air without a sound, carrying only the chill of a world long dead.

Above, the sky flickers with the eerie glow of the aurora borealis, casting ghostly light over the frozen plains. It's a beauty that offers no comfort, just a cold reminder of how far this realm is from anything alive. The ice groans beneath, ancient and shifting, while massive glaciers loom like jagged sentinels, their sharp edges a warning to any who might venture too close.

Buried deep beneath it all lies Ymir, the primordial giant, frozen in the ice, a relic from a time of unimaginable power. His colossal form is trapped, held in place by the unyielding frost, a memory of a force that once moved the stars but now lies still. His presence lingers, like a dream that won't fade, a reminder of something ancient and terrible, encased in eternal cold.

## Weather (d6)

1. **Blinding Snowstorm**: A blizzard rages across the wasteland, reducing visibility to nothing and freezing everything in its path. The party must Test Fortitude DR14 to endure the cold or take d4 damage for every hour spent in the storm.
2. **Freezing Mist**: The air grows heavy with a freezing mist that softens the harsh environment. The mist obscures vision, giving the party advantage on Tests that rely on stealth for the next day.
3. **Ice Shards**: Sharp ice shards rain down from the sky, carried by the wind. The party must Test Swift DR12 to avoid taking d4 damage from the falling shards. Finding shelter is crucial, as the storm could last for d4 hours.
4. **Aurora's Blessing**: The aurora borealis dances across the sky, its vibrant lights energising the party. Any character who takes a moment to bask in its beauty feels their degraded armour repair itself.
5. **Frozen Ground**: The ground beneath the party cracks and shifts, revealing icy fissures that make movement dangerous. They must Test Swift DR12 to avoid slipping into a crevasse.
6. **Silent Night**: An unnatural silence descends on Niflheim, muting all sound. Communication is impossible, and the party's footsteps leave no echo. This eerie calm puts everyone on edge.

## Discoveries (d6)

1. **Frozen Relic**: The party uncovers a Rune, encased in ice, glowing faintly. Test Wits DR12 to avoid setting off an ancient trap (d8 damage) embedded within the ice.
2. **Ymir's Blood**: The ice cracks open to reveal a pool of Ymir's blood, frozen yet glowing with raw power. Drinking it grants +2 Might for d4 hours but at a steep price—using it drains d6HP, as your body fights to contain the primordial strength coursing through you.
3. **Frozen Lake of the Dead**: A frozen lake, its surface covered with the bodies of the dead, stretches before the party. Crossing it risks disturbing the restless spirits below, who may rise if the ice is disturbed. Test Wits DR14 to avoid awakening the dead and inciting a violent attack.
4. **Glacial Cavern**: The party stumbles upon a hidden cavern of ice, filled with statues of ancient warriors, preserved in time. Some may carry Runes, while others might be the trapped souls of warriors, waiting for a new body to claim.
5. **Ancient Sigils**: Ancient runic sigils are carved into the ice, their meanings undiscernable. Test Wits DR12 to study them for insights into Niflheim's dark magic and to gain a random Rune.
6. **Frost-Rimed Scroll**: Buried deep beneath layers of ice, the party finds a Rune—its symbol glowing with ancient power. To decipher its meaning, the party must Test Wits DR12 to avoid triggering a wrath from the gods.

## Rumours (d6)

1. "Ymir stirs." Whispers say that Ymir, the primordial giant, stirs beneath the frozen earth. Some say his awakening will mark the end of the world, others believe his ancient power can be bent to their will.
2. "Hrimgrimnir is gathering an army." Hrimgrimnir, the Icebound Jarl, is said to be assembling an army of frost spectres and ice wraiths, preparing to invade Asgard. Any who venture too close to his fortress vanish without a trace.
3. "The River Serpents are restless." The rivers of Hvergelmir are rising, and the River Serpents are becoming more aggressive. There are whispers of something hidden deep within the frozen waters—Trinkets or a powerful Rune, perhaps—guarded fiercely by the serpents.
4. "The Mistwalkers are multiplying." Once rare, the Mistwalkers now appear in greater numbers. They are drawn to the places where the veil between worlds thins, preying on the lost and the weak, luring them into endless fog and confusion.
5. "The dead are restless." The frozen dead are rising with increasing frequency, their spirits unable to find rest in Niflheim's cold wasteland. Some say the realm itself is awakening, growing more hostile to all who dare tread its frozen plains.
6. "Ymir's heart still beats." Deep within the ice, it's said that Ymir's heart still beats—a slow, rhythmic pulse felt by those who venture near. Some believe that touching it will grant them his power, while others think it will bring nothing but destruction.

## Encounters (d8)

1. **Frost Spectre Attack**: d4 Frost Spectres drift toward the party. Their touch freezes the air, chilling the bones before they even lay a hand on you.
2. **Ice Wraith Swarm**: A swarm of d8 Ice Wraiths slides silently through the mist, their forms barely visible in the frigid air. The party must Test Wits DR12 to spot them. Failure results in the wraiths sapping d6HP before battle.
3. **Mistwalker Trap**: The party is surrounded by d4 Mistwalkers, shifting beings of fog and fear. Their presence disorientates the party. They must Test Wits DR14 to maintain their bearings, or risk walking straight into a death trap of shifting mists.
4. **River Serpent Attack**: Near the frozen rivers of Hvergelmir, d2 River Serpents strike from beneath the ice. The party must Test Swift DR12 to dodge their deadly bites, or suffer d6 damage as the serpents' venomous chill freezes their limbs, reducing movement for d4 rounds.
5. **Cracking Ice**: As the party crosses the frozen plains, the ice beneath them begins to crack. Test Swift DR12 to avoid falling into a frozen crevasse. Anyone who fails is plunged into the freezing waters below, suffering d6 cold damage and must Test Fortitude DR14 or face a fever from the frigid touch.
6. **Frozen Corpse**: The party comes across a corpse in the ice—an adventurer, or perhaps a giant, long dead from the frozen grip of Niflheim. Searching the body may yield d3 Runes, hidden among the decay.
7. **Beckoning Sound**: An eerie sound, and what looks like soldiers clad in black ice, marching through the wasteland. They are emotionless, void of expression. Then without question, they vanish, an echo of their time in life.
8. **Ymir's Awakening**: The ice beneath the party cracks as they venture deeper into Niflheim. Ymir, the primordial giant, stirs in his frozen tomb. The party must Test Wits DR14 to escape the shifting ice or risk being dragged into the depths of his ancient prison, where his cold power lingers.

## Creatures of Niflheim

### Frost Spectres
- **HP:** 5
- **Armour:** Deceptive movement - d2
- **Weapon:** Ice shard nails d6
- **Special:** Fire Weakness. Takes double damage from fire-based attacks.

### Ice Wraiths
- **HP:** 4
- **Armour:** -
- **Weapon:** Freezing touch d4
- **Special:** Incorporeal. Ignores physical attacks unless made with Rune-enchanted weapons or fire.

### Mistwalkers
- **HP:** 6
- **Armour:** Misty form - d2
- **Weapon:** Withered claws d6
- **Special:** Claws of Confusion. On a hit, the target must Test Wits DR12 or experience temporary hallucinations, imposing a +2 DR to their next attack. Shifting Form. Attacks against the Mistwalker are DR+1.

### River Serpents
- **HP:** 10
- **Armour:** Permafrost scales - d3
- **Weapon:** Frozen fangs d8
- **Special:** Aquatic Ambush. If the serpent attacks from the water, the target's defence is made with disadvantage.

### Ymir, the Primordial Giant
- **HP:** 40
- **Armour:** Frost-hardened flesh - d4
- **Weapon:** Primordial breath d6+2 ranged (ignoring non-enchanted armour)
- **Special:** Ancient Wind. Ymir's Primordial Breath can hit multiple targets and freezes non-magical items on a fumble, shattering them. Awaken the Depths. Every round, Ymir's presence causes the ice beneath to shift and crack. All characters must Test Swift DR14 at the start of their turn or take d6 cold damage as the ice shifts.
- **Reward:** Creator's Gift. Activate once to bring the mischievous legion to its knees, dealing double damage to followers of Loki for d4 rounds.

### Hrimgrimnir, the Icebound Jarl
- **HP:** 28
- **Armour:** Black ice armour - d3
- **Weapon:** Icebound greatsword d10+3
- **Special:** Unholy Frost. Hrimgrimnir wields a massive greatsword imbued with an unholy frost. On a fumble, the target is encased in ice and must Test Fortitude DR14 to break free or be stunned for d4 rounds. Frost Legion. Hrimgrimnir can summon d4 FrostBound Warriors (d4HP, Ice axe d6) to his side each round, bound to his command until defeated.
- **Reward:** Icebound Greatsword (d10+3 damage). Once per session, unleash a storm of hail dealing d8 damage to all enemies you can see.
