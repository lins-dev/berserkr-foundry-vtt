# Asgard: Realm of the Aesir

Asgard, once a realm of divine order and beauty, now lies under gloomy, storm-filled skies. Fimbulwinter has seeped into the gods' realm, distorting its once-majestic landscape. Valhalla stands battered by fierce winds, lightning tearing across the sky, as if the heavens themselves mourn the coming of Ragnarök.

The Bifrost flickers weakly, its light fading under the weight of the impending apocalypse. Forests groan under torrential rain, and the land feels brittle, mirroring the turmoil gripping the gods. Once united, Asgard's deities are now fractured, whispers of fear and distrust replacing the laughter and merriment that once echoed through the halls.

Tempests have turned Asgard into a storm-filled battlefield, where even gods must tread carefully. The river Ifing is frozen, allowing Jotnar to invade the heart of the divine realm. With Ragnarök looming, the gods face a stark reality: their time, like that of mortals, is running out.

## Weather (d6)

1. **Divine Storm**: A storm born of Thor's fury tears across the sky, its lightning striking wildly. Each bolt threatens the party, dealing d6 damage to anyone it hits. But amidst the chaos, there's a 1-in-6 chance a strike charges a weapon nearby, granting it +2 lightning damage for the next battle.
2. **Aurora Veil**: An aurora dances across the stormy Asgardian sky, painting the land in flickering shades of green, blue, and gold. Those who stop to behold its beauty feel a sudden clarity, as if touched by a lingering divine presence.
3. **Frozen Twilight**: The sun vanishes, leaving Asgard shrouded in a twilight. For the next d4 hours, the unnatural gloom muffles sight and sound, granting advantage on all Tests for stealth.
4. **Sunlit Cold**: A beam of golden light pierces the clouds, bathing Asgard in rare warmth and granting all within it +1 to Fortitude Tests for the next day. Yet this fleeting reprieve from the cold does not go unnoticed—divine eyes turn toward the party, and a challenge may soon follow.
5. **Hail of Spears**: A hailstorm of divine spears rains down, the sky itself bracing for Ragnarök. The ground is soon strewn with shattered, golden weapons. Test Swift DR12 to avoid them or take d6 damage.
6. **Fimbulwinter's Gale**: A howling gale tears through Asgard, its breath claiming all in its path. Anyone exposed for more than an hour must roll d4 and subtract the result from their food. Any magical fire is extinguished by the relentless wind.

## Discoveries (d6)

1. **Idunn's Fallen Apple**: A lone golden apple rests untouched in the terrain, its glow a promise of vitality. Eating it restores full health and grants +1 to all Tests for the rest of the day. But such a gift is not without risk—taking it may invoke the wrath of the gods or catch the eyes of the ever-vigilant Idunn's Protectors.
2. **Shattered Valkyrie Spear**: The shattered remains of a Valkyrie's spear lie on the ground, faintly glowing with divine energy. Though broken, its power lingers. Reforging it, with the aid of a Dwarf or a smith, could restore it as a weapon capable of granting +2 to all Might Tests against Jotnar.
3. **Runestone of Prophecy**: A cluster of d3 Runes juts from the rocks, their stone surfaces cracked and weathered. The faint carvings pulse with ancient energy, offering fleeting visions of Ragnarok to those who dare to touch them.
4. **Einherjar Helm**: A battered helmet from a fallen Einherjar warrior rests on the ground, its surface scarred by countless battles. Wearing it grants +2 to defence but binds the wearer to the warrior's unfinished business. Each time they rest, they'll endure vivid dreams of Ragnarok, the visions growing more intense with time. Halve all healing from rests until the curse is lifted.
5. **Forseti's Abandoned Scale**: Amid the ruins, the party discovers a massive golden scale, once belonging to Forseti, the god of justice. One side remains tipped, eternally weighing a forgotten decision. If taken, the scale whispers to the owner that they must take vengeance whenever they are wronged.
6. **Golden Mead Flask**: The party discovers a flask of mead blessed by the gods, its golden liquid radiating warmth. Drinking it restores 2d6HP and gives +2 to all Tests when flying. The drinker must Test Wits DR12 to resist the mead's deeper effects—failure leaves the drinker haunted by a chilling glimpse of their own death during Ragnarok, suffering a -2 to all Tests made in taverns or while drinking alcohol.

## Rumours (d6)

1. "Loki walks among us." Whispers grow that Loki has infiltrated Asgard in disguise, sowing discord among the Aesir. Some say she's already taken control of the Bifrost. Many fear his machinations will shatter Asgard's defences further, leaving the realm dangerously exposed to the Jotnar's advance.
2. "Odin is watching everyone." The gods murmur of Odin's rising paranoia, his trust worn thin by the weight of Ragnarok. Huginn and Muninn now watch not just Asgard's enemies but his own allies. Those who defy his will or act against his interests risk being marked for swift and merciless judgement.
3. "The Valkyries choose no more." Whispers spread that the Valkyries have ceased choosing the worthy for Valhalla. Some say they've lost faith in the gods' power to halt Ragnarok, while others fear they're preparing for something darker—an unseen purpose that could shake the realms.
4. "The Bifrost is cracking." Rumours circulate of the Bifrost's weakening. Its once-steady glow now flickers, unstable and frail. Some say even Heimdall strains to keep it intact. Soon, crossing the bridge may become perilous—even for the gods themselves.
5. "A traitor walks among us." Rumours swirl that one of the Aesir has betrayed Asgard, secretly aligning with Loki. Freyr and Tyr draw the most suspicion, though none dare voice their fears openly. The air between the gods grows heavier, tension threatening to fracture their fragile unity.
6. "Thor's hammer has been seen in the wrong hands." Whispers circulate that Mjolnir, Thor's legendary hammer, was spotted in the hands of a mortal. Some say it is a sign that the gods are losing control, while others believe it to be a divine test. Whether stolen, gifted, or lost, its absence from Thor's side has left the gods uneasy, fearing what havoc it could unleash if wielded against them.

## Encounters (d8)

1. **Heimdall's Challenge**: The party encounters Heimdall, the watchman of the gods, at the base of the Bifrost. He demands they prove their worthiness to enter Asgard, challenging them to a test of vigilance or a riddle. Failure to impress them seals certain paths, but success earns safe passage—or even a blessing from Heimdall himself.
2. **Valkyrie's Judgement**: A Valkyrie descends, her eyes blazing, her presence heavy with judgement. She demands to know if the party is worthy of her respect, her voice like steel. She challenges the strongest looking member to a holmgang. If they show valour, she may bestow guidance, her blessing as rare as it is powerful.
3. **Einherjar Patrol**: A patrol of d6 Einherjar, Valhalla's immortal warriors, strides through the realm, their eyes set for battle. They approach the party, offering a trial of combat alongside them against Jotunn invaders. Victory may earn a favour from Odin, but refusal risks the Aesir's wrath, with whispers of cowardice lingering in their wake.
4. **Hrist's Fury**: Hrist, a fallen Valkyrie with bloodstained wings, emerges from the shadows, her once-divine purpose lost to madness. No longer serving the gods, she hunts mortals for sport, stalking the party with predatory patience. If they endure her relentless attacks, they may uncover the tragedy that drove her to forsake her role.
5. **Vali, The Avenger**: Vali, the god of vengeance, steps forward, his presence sharp. He demands the party right a wrong—be it personal or divine—with no room for negotiation. His tone carries the weight of inevitability: act now or face his wrath. Agreeing may earn his favour, but refusal guarantees the relentless wrath of his justice.
6. **Idunn's Orchard**: The party discovers a hidden grove of Idunn's apples, their golden glow a promise of vitality. A PC who eats one is fully healed and receives +1 to all Tests for the rest of the day. But such power comes with risk—plucking an apple may draw the attention of Loki or perhaps worse, Idunn's Protectors.
7. **The Shattered Bifrost**: The Bifrost lies fractured, the once-glorious bridge between realms splintered by Fimbulwinter. Among the ruins, the party finds shards crackling with divine energy. These can be used for a one-time teleportation to another realm. However, the magic is unstable—using a shard requires you to Test Wits DR14. Failure means that you are transported to an unintended and potentially dangerous realm.
8. **The Allfather's Eye**: Huginn, Odin's great raven, lands nearby, its piercing gaze fixed on the party. It watches in silence, a test of their resolve. If they fall short, the raven croaks a cryptic warning of impending doom before taking flight. If they prove worthy, Huginn leaves behind a Rune, a gift from the Allfather.

## Creatures of Asgard

### Vakten
- **HP:** 7
- **Armour:** Asgardian armour - d2
- **Weapon:** Asgardian axe d6
- **Special:** Vakten Horn. Once blown (foregoing an attack), Fates cannot be used during the next round.

### Valkyries
- **HP:** 12
- **Armour:** Golden Armour - d2
- **Weapon:** Sword d6, Spear d8 ranged
- **Special:** Fly. Can fly at will.

### Einherjar
- **HP:** 8
- **Armour:** -
- **Weapon:** Varied weapons. Roll d6: 1-2=Throwing Axe d4 ranged, 3-4=Morning Star d6, 5-6=Lance d8
- **Special:** Insult to Injury. Attacks deal +2 damage to targets already wounded.

### Idunn's Protectors
- **HP:** 5
- **Armour:** Golden bark - d3
- **Weapon:** Thorns of Idunn d6
- **Special:** Regeneration. Heals d3HP at the end of every round.

### Hrist, Valkyrie of Fury
- **HP:** 18
- **Armour:** Golden armour - d3
- **Weapon:** Golden longsword d8+3
- **Special:** Fly. Can fly at will. Amnesia. You will not remember this encounter.
- **Rewards:** Golden Longsword (d8+3 damage).

### Vali, The Avenger
- **HP:** 27
- **Armour:** -
- **Weapon:** Avenger's greataxe d10+3
- **Special:** Bloodied. Once at half health or below, attacks and defence are DR+2.
- **Rewards:** The Avenger's Greataxe (d10+3 damage).
