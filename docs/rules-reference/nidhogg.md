# Nidhogg, the Corpse Eater

Nidhogg should feel relentless. His attacks sap strength, his abilities corrupt and trap, and his presence is a constant drain on the party's resources. The fight becomes increasingly dire as he enters his final phase, embodying the inevitable decay he represents.

## Running Nidhogg
- **Corruption First:** Start with Breath of Decay to weaken the party, making every subsequent attack hit harder.
- **Control the Fight:** Use The Roots of Yggdrasil to trap key characters and reduce their ability to defend or attack effectively.
- **Finish with Fury:** In Death's Awakening, turn up the intensity. His regeneration and increased damage force characters to focus their attacks or risk losing momentum in the fight.

## Loot and Rewards
- **Fang of Nidhogg:** A sharp, necrotic fang that can be fashioned into a weapon. It deals d10+4 damage and causes an additional d6 damage per round for d4 rounds unless healed by magic.
- **Nidhogg's Scales:** Thick, blackened scales that can be crafted into armour. Armour made from these scales grants +3 armour.
- **Blessing of Nidhogg:** The blessing grants +2 to all Tests when fighting undead or creatures from the underworld. Once per day, the character can summon necrotic roots to entangle enemies (DR14 to resist, d8 damage per round).

## Attacks

### Corrupting Bite
- **Damage:** d12+4
- **Effects:** On a defence fumble, Nidhogg's bite corrupts the target. They must strike the tale they are proudest of from their Saga.

### Tail Whip
- **Damage:** d8+3
- **Effects:** Nidhogg sweeps his tail across the battlefield, forcing all enemies to Test Swift DR12 or take d8+3 damage and be knocked prone.

### Necrotic Breath
- **Damage:** d10+4 ranged
- **Effects:** Nidhogg breathes a concentrated stream of necrotic energy, targeting a single enemy. If the target fails a Fortitude Test DR14, they take d10+4 damage and suffer d6 damage for the next d4 rounds.

## Special

### Breath of Decay
- **Uses:** Once Per Battle
- **Effect:** Nidhogg exhales a cloud of necrotic energy that causes rapid decay in anything it touches, corrupting both flesh and metal. All enemies must Test Fortitude DR14 or take 2d8 damage. If affected, enemies also suffer -2 to all Might Tests for d4 rounds as the decay saps their strength. Non-enchanted armour caught in the cloud is also weakened, reducing its effectiveness by 1 tier.

### Deathly Stare
- **Uses:** Once Per Battle
- **Effect:** Nidhogg can peer into the souls of his enemies. The Corpse Eater stares at an enemy and they must Test Wits DR14 to avoid the gaze. If failed, the target gains +4 to attacks and must attack their most trusted ally. The effects last for d3 rounds.

### Soul Drain
- **Uses:** Once Per Battle
- **Effect:** Nidhogg feeds on the life force of his enemies, draining their vitality to sustain his own power. Nidhogg targets one enemy and drains their life force. The target must Test Fortitude DR16 or take 3d6 damage. Nidhogg regains the damage dealt as HP.

## Death's Awakening
- **Activates at:** HP < 25
- When Nidhogg is close to death, his connection to the underworld strengthens, making him even more dangerous as he taps into the power of the dead.
  - Nidhogg gains an additional +3 to all defence Tests.
  - He regenerates d6HP per round (to a maximum of 25HP).
  - All of Nidhogg's attacks during this time deal an additional d6 damage.
  - While in this state, Nidhogg is immune to all conditions.

### The Roots of Yggdrasil
- Nidhogg channels the dark, corrupted power of Yggdrasil, summoning deadly roots that entangle and strangle his enemies. He can do this only once. Nidhogg calls forth roots from the ground, targeting up to 3 enemies. They must Test Swift DR14 to avoid being entangled. If entangled, the targets take d8 damage each round and must Test Might DR14 to break free. The roots drain life energy, causing the entangled targets to suffer -2 to all Tests for the duration of the effect. The roots last for d4 rounds, after which they wither and die.
