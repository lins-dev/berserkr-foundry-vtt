# Surtr, the Fire Giant

Surtr should feel like a living inferno. His attacks and abilities overwhelm characters with fire and destruction, forcing constant adaptation. His final phase embodies the fiery fury of Muspelheim, turning the fight into a desperate race against time.

## Running Surtr
- **Control the Battlefield:** Use Firestorm and Lava Eruption early to scatter and weaken the party, forcing tactical movement.
- **Punish Close Combat:** Leverage Unquenchable Flame to deter melee combatants, while delivering devastating blows with Flaming Cleave.
- **Final Blaze:** In Fireborn Fury, focus on relentless attacks, pushing the characters to finish the battle quickly or risk being overwhelmed by Surtr's flames.

## Loot and Rewards
- **Sword of Surtr:** This massive flaming sword deals d12+6 damage and deals an additional d6 fire damage to every strike. Once per day, the wielder can use it to summon a Firestorm, dealing 2d8 fire damage to all enemies they can see.
- **Molten Armour:** Surtr's armour can be crafted into molten plate that grants +3 armour and immunity to fire damage. Additionally, anyone who strikes the wearer in melee takes d6 fire damage.
- **Surtr's Essence:** A vial of Surtr's essence can be consumed to grant the user resistance to fire damage (taking only half damage) and +2 to Might Tests for one battle per day. However, after the battle, the user takes d6 fire damage due to the burning energy coursing through their body.

## Attacks

### Flaming Cleave
- **Damage:** d12+6
- **Effects:** Surtr swings his massive flaming sword in a wide arc, hitting multiple enemies. On a defence fumble, the target's weapon or armour catches fire, requiring a Test Swift DR12 to extinguish or take d6 fire damage each round.

### Scorching Gaze
- **Damage:** d10+3 ranged
- **Effects:** Surtr's eyes burn with the fire of Muspelheim. He can focus his gaze on a single target, dealing d10 fire damage. Test Guile DR14 or be blinded for d3 rounds.

## Special

### Firestorm
- **Uses:** Once Per Battle
- **Effect:** Surtr can summon a firestorm that rains down flames across the battlefield, igniting everything in sight. All enemies he can see must Test Fortitude DR14 or take 2d8 fire damage. The battlefield is then covered in flames for the next d4 rounds, causing any movement through the area to deal d6 fire damage to characters and reducing movement.

### Lava Eruption
- **Uses:** Once Per Battle
- **Effect:** Surtr calls upon the power of Muspelheim, causing lava to erupt from beneath the battlefield, creating pools of molten rock. Surtr targets a section of the battlefield, causing lava to erupt and form pools. All enemies in the area must Test Swift DR14 to avoid the eruption. If they fail, they take 3d6 fire damage and are knocked prone. The pools of lava remain for the rest of the battle, forcing characters to Test Swift DR12 to avoid stepping in them, taking d6 fire damage on a failure.

### Unquenchable Flame
- **Uses:** Passive
- **Effect:** Surtr's body is covered in unquenchable flames, making him nearly impossible to approach without being scorched. Any enemy who strikes Surtr in melee takes d2 fire damage automatically, as the heat radiates from his body. Surtr is immune to all fire-based damage and takes half damage from any magical attacks not related to water or cold.

## Fireborn Fury
- **Activates at:** HP < 25
- When Surtr is severely injured, he enters a state of fiery fury, drawing upon the eternal flames of Muspelheim to fuel his final assault.
  - When Surtr's HP drops below 25, he attacks twice per round.
  - He imposes an additional +2 to all the PCs' defence Tests.
  - He deals an additional d6 fire damage on all of his attacks.
  - Surtr also regenerates d6HP per round for d4 rounds (to a maximum of 25HP), as the flames of Muspelheim restore his strength.
  - During this phase, Surtr's Unquenchable Flame ability deals double damage to anyone who strikes him in melee.

### Ragnarök's Blaze
- Surtr ignites the entire battlefield in a cataclysmic blaze. He can do this only once. Surtr plunges his sword into the ground, causing flames to erupt across the battlefield. All enemies must Test Fortitude DR16 or take 2d8 fire damage and be set on fire (d6 fire damage per round).
