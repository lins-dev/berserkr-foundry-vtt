# Alfheim: Realm of the Light Elves

Alfheim, the land that used to glow like something out of a dream, is now nothing but a scorched desert. No snow, no cold—just an endless stretch of sandstorms that tear through what's left of this parched place. The once-brilliant sky? It's nothing but a dull haze, choking out the light like the last drag of a cigarette. The golden cities, the shining towers the Elves built to touch the heavens? Buried. Gone. The sands swallowed them up like they never existed.

Upon the surface, the desert is alive with things that shouldn't exist. Giant scorpions and sand serpents—they're the new kings here, slithering under the dunes, waiting to strike. And if the creatures don't get you, the spirits will. Ghosts of the past wander the wasteland, beautiful, terrible things, drawing you in with promises of what once was. But it's all a lie, and before you know it you're just another body buried beneath the sand.

Alfheim might have escaped the ice, but it sure as hell didn't escape the end. The light's gone, buried under layers of dust and regret. And as Ragnarok looms, the Elves are left wondering if they'll ever see the stars again—or if their once-brilliant world will just disappear, forgotten, like so many others before it.

## Weather (d6)

1. **Eternal Horizon**: The desert stretches on forever, the heat shimmering off the sand like a curse. There's something eerie about it, like Alfheim itself is lost in time.
2. **Blinding Sandstorm**: The sandstorm hits fast, fierce. Visibility drops to nothing. All Tests that rely on perception are made with disadvantage as the sand grinds against your skin. Anyone caught outside takes d4 damage every hour from the abrasiveness of the wind. Moving through it? You're blind, and you're walking through hell.
3. **Echoes of Glory**: The wind carries something. Almost like music—melodic fragments of a world long gone. The air hums with the memory of Alfheim's lost grandeur, but it's a ghost now.
4. **Sudden Drought**: The air turns thick, dry. Water in your packs vanishes into nothing (lose d4 water), evaporated by the unnatural heat. Test Wits DR14 to find a water source before dehydration kicks in. Fail, and you'll be feeling the sun drain every drop from you.
5. **Fleeting Mirage**: A shimmer on the horizon. An oasis, maybe a city, or a figure—someone offering help. You can't help but be drawn to it. Test Wits DR12 to resist the pull. If you fail, you'll chase the mirage, and when you get there? Just more sand, more nothing.
6. **Light's Last Gasp**: For a moment, the sky glows. A last flicker of Alfheim's beauty, and for an instant, everything feels... alive. You gain a +2 to Wits Tests for the next hour. But that beauty fades fast, leaving a deep, aching sense of loss. Like it's the last breath of a dying world.

## Discoveries (d6)

1. **Buried Elven Relic**: The sands have swallowed it, but a faint magical glow shines through. A random Rune, but don't get too comfortable. Its power's nearly spent, and trying to tap into it for too long might trigger a magical mishap that'll leave you burned or worse.
2. **Crumbling Tower**: The skeleton of an elven tower juts out from the dunes like a dying beast. Inside, ancient Runes and long-forgotten Trinkets wait to be uncovered. But the tower's fragile. Test Wits DR14 before disturbing anything, or risk it collapsing and trapping you under a pile of stone.
3. **Shattered Mirror of Light**: Once a conduit for the Elves to harness light's pure power, the mirror is now shattered, its pieces scattered across the sands. If you're clever enough to reassemble it, the mirror might offer the location of something desired.
4. **Sunken Elven Temple**: A submerged temple, hidden beneath the ever-shifting sands. Sheltered from the sun, it's a place to rest. But inside, the air is thick with magic, and old Runes guard the temple. You might uncover some elven secrets or just unleash something best left undisturbed.
5. **Desiccated Corpse**: A corpse, dried out by the sun, lies in the sand. Maybe it was a fool who didn't know when to stop, maybe someone searching for the same answers you are. On them, there's a map or key to an elven vault, but what's waiting for you at the end of that path? Treasure? Or an early grave?
6. **Glowing Oasis**: A flicker of light in the middle of the godforsaken desert—an oasis that glows under the harsh sun. It offers water and a chance to recover, letting you regain d6HP. But nothing comes without a price in this place. Mirage Stalkers or worse might be lurking, waiting to see if you're worth killing.

## Rumours (d6)

1. "The last city is buried." The final city of Alfheim's glory got swallowed up by the sands, its magic still flickering deep beneath the dust. Some say it's just waiting to be unleashed, while others call it a cursed tomb—untouchable, best left alone. But who really listens to that kind of advice in a place like this?
2. "The Mirage Stalkers are growing bolder." Once rare, those souls—the Mirage Stalkers—are everywhere now. They're drawn to magic like flies to shit, hunting down anyone with Rune-inscribed weapons or armour. They're not as easy to outrun anymore.
3. "Elfric is looking for allies." Elfric, the noble from the Luminous Court, is out there hunting for people dumb enough—or desperate enough—to help him save what's left of Alfheim's fading magic. Word is, he's willing to spill Aesir secrets if you prove you're trustworthy.
4. "The Gatekeeper is watching." Eirwyn's still out there, guarding the last damn gate to somewhere untouched by the eternal winter. She's not one to be charmed or fooled. You'll need to pass her tests—prove you're worth the trouble.
5. "The sands are cursed." They say the sands of Alfheim are cursed, soaked in the souls of those poor bastards who died in the storms. Dig too deep and you might awaken something hungry. Some old vengeful foe with a bone to pick.
6. "The desert hides something." They say buried under the dunes is an elven weapon—something powerful enough to either light up the world or burn it all down. Some think it's their last shot at salvation, others—well, they just hope it doesn't end up in the wrong hands.

## Encounters (d8)

1. **Sun-Touched Raiders**: These mad, desperate raiders attack without warning. d6 Sun-Touched Raiders demand supplies or ambush you for loot.
2. **Sand Worm Ambush**: The ground rumbles, the air thickens. A massive Sand Worm bursts from the dunes, devouring everything in its path. Test Swift DR14 to dodge the maw, or be swallowed whole. Its hide is as tough as iron, and you've got to strike hard if you want to hurt it.
3. **Mirage Trappers**: A figure flickers on the horizon—a lost traveller or a safe haven. Or so you think. As you near, the truth shows itself. It's d4 Mirage Trappers, deadly predators of sand and light. Test Wits DR12 to see through the illusion before they strike. If you fail, you're caught off guard, and they get a free attack before initiative.
4. **Desert Scorpions**: They wait beneath the sand, venomous and deadly. d4 Desert Scorpions burst from below, their stingers striking with deadly speed, and if you're not fast, you'll be paralyzed by their poison. Test Swift DR14 to dodge their strike, or you'll be feeling the sting.
5. **Spirit of a Fallen City**: The ruins of a long-forgotten Alfheim city stand half-buried in the sand. There's something here, tempting you with glimpses of the past, but it's a trap. Test Wits DR14 to resist the lure. Follow too close and the spirit will gobble you right into the quicksand.
6. **Alfric of the Luminous Court**: Alfric, a noble of Alfheim's broken court, offers you a quest: find a relic, restore a spark of the old glory. But his trust is hard to win. Fail his task, and he'll turn on you faster than a desert wind.
7. **Eirwyn the Gatekeeper**: The last guardian of Alfheim's gates, standing between you and travel to the realms beyond. Whenever the party tries to travel to a different realm Eirwyn will show up to judge their worth.
8. **Sandstorm Surge**: A sudden sandstorm swallows everything. The air turns black. Test Fortitude DR14 to hold on, or get lost in the blinding winds and lose half of your supplies (food, water and Trinkets).

## Creatures of Alfheim

### Sun-Touched Raiders
- **HP:** 6
- **Armour:** -
- **Weapon:** Varied weapons. Roll d6: 1-2=Broken spear shaft d4, 3-4=Rusty scimitar d6, 5-6=Desert hook d8
- **Special:** Mug. When a Sun-Touched Raider successfully attacks, they have a 50% chance of stealing one random item from the target. If successful, they immediately retreat into the sand, vanishing with the stolen item.

### Sand Worms
- **HP:** 11
- **Armour:** Shield-sized scales - d3
- **Weapon:** Crushing bite d8
- **Special:** Swallow. On a fumble, the Sand Worm swallows a target whole. The swallowed character takes d6 damage each round and must pass a Might Test DR14 to break free. Other party members can also attack the worm's soft underbelly from outside DR12 to release the swallowed character.

### Mirage Trappers
- **HP:** 4
- **Armour:** -
- **Weapon:** Blades of sand d6
- **Special:** Multi-Mirage. Due to their ethereal form, Mirage Trappers can only be hit on attacks of DR15 or higher unless the attacker is using magical or rune enchanted weapons, DR10.

### Desert Scorpions
- **HP:** 5
- **Armour:** Chitin plates - d3
- **Weapon:** Poisonous stinger d6
- **Special:** Poison Stinger. When the Desert Scorpion's stinger hits, the target must Test Fortitude DR12 or become poisoned.

### Alfric of the Luminous Court
- **HP:** 18
- **Armour:** Reflective armour - d3
- **Weapon:** Luminous spear d8+2
- **Special:** Mirror Shield. Once per encounter, Alfric can use his Mirror Shield to reflect a spell or ranged attack back at its caster. The attacker takes the original damage or effect they intended for Alfric. Light Blindness. When Alfric activates this ability, his spear emits an intense, blinding flash of light. All enemies that can see the spear must Test Wits DR14 or be temporarily blinded for one round, increasing their attack DR by +4 during that time.
- **Reward:** The Light of the Court. Activate once to blind all enemies that can see you for d3 rounds.

### Eirwyn the Gatekeeper
- **HP:** 24
- **Armour:** Alfheim heavy armour - d3
- **Weapon:** Gatekeeper's warhammer d10+3
- **Special:** Gatekeeper's Retribution. When an enemy successfully hits Eirwyn, she automatically counters with a retaliatory strike, dealing d6 damage back to the attacker. This ability activates once per round. Unyielding Threshold. Eirwyn can create a magical barrier that blocks any enemy trying to cross into Alfheim without her permission. Enemies must Test Might DR16 to bypass the barrier, or they are forcibly repelled, taking d4 damage.
- **Reward:** Gatekeeper's Lock. Speak a Name and a Realm once. The named may not enter the realm for d6 days.
