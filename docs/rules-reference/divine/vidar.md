# Vidar, God of Vengeance

Vidar is a god of raw power, embodying the inevitability of vengeance. His attacks hit with crushing finality, and his presence on the battlefield demands respect. Facing Vidar is not just a fight—it's a reckoning, one that forces the party to match his unstoppable will or be shattered beneath it.

## Running Vidar
- **Brutal Precision:** Vidar's attacks should feel heavy and deliberate. Use Crushing Stomp early to destabilise the party, forcing them to regroup while Vidar punishes any missteps with his sword or fists.
- **Vengeance Unleashed:** Lean into Vengeful Strike when Vidar suffers a significant blow, or if the party has already killed an Aesir god, when the time feels right.
- **Final Fury:** As Vidar approaches low health, his Indomitable Will transforms the battlefield into a desperate struggle for survival. His increased damage and double attacks make him a terrifying presence that demands focus.

## Loot and Rewards
- **Vidar's Blade:** A massive sword that deals d12+6 damage. Once per day, it can ignore armour and deal an additional 2d12 damage on a single strike. After that strike, you become fatigued.
- **Iron Shoe of Vidar:** This iron shoe grants the wearer +2 armour and allows them to perform a Crushing Stomp. Once per day, Test Might DR12 to deal d8 damage to all enemies close to you and also knock them prone.
- **Blessing of Vengeance:** This blessing allows the character to gain +4 to a single attack when striking back after a significant loss or injury. Once per day, when reduced to 10HP or lower, the character can make a Vengeful Strike, dealing 2d12 damage to an enemy that hurt them or their ally.

## Attacks

### Vidar's Blade
- **Damage:** d12+2
- **Effects:** Vidar's massive sword deals devastating damage, cutting through most armour with ease. On a defence fumble armour is degraded an additional tier.

### Fist of Vengeance
- **Damage:** d8+4
- **Effects:** Vidar's unarmed strike is powerful enough to crush bones. If the target is stunned or knocked prone, this attack deals an additional d8 damage.

## Special

### Crushing Stomp
- **Uses:** Once Per Battle
- **Effect:** With his massive iron shoe, Vidar delivers a crushing blow that reverberates across the battlefield. Vidar stomps the ground or his target, causing an earthquake or crushing blow.
  - **Ground:** The stomp causes the ground to quake, forcing all enemies close to him to Test Swift DR14 or be knocked prone and take d6 damage from the shock wave.
  - **Target:** Deals 2d8 damage to a single enemy and knocks them prone.

### Vengeful Strike
- **Uses:** Once Per Battle
- **Effect:** If any other Aesir god has been killed or he is struck down himself, his rage empowers him to unleash a devastating attack. Vidar gains +4 to his next attack and deals 2d12+4 damage on his next successful hit, which can only be activated if:
  - He has taken more than 12 damage in a single hit.
  - An Aesir god has been felled by the party.

### Unstoppable Force
- **Uses:** Passive
- **Effect:** Vidar's sheer strength makes him nearly impossible to slow down or stop in battle. Vidar cannot be stunned or knocked prone by normal means. Any attempts automatically fail unless they involve runic magic or inscribed weapons.

## Indomitable Will
- **Activates at:** HP < 15
- When Vidar is on the verge of defeat, his resolve and physical strength only increase.
  - Vidar gains +2 armour.
  - He gains +4 to all damage rolls.
  - Vidar attacks twice per round, pushing forward with raw, unrelenting vengeance.

### Fenrir's Bane
- Destined to bring down the wolf Fenrir, Vidar channels this same unstoppable strength into a single, monumental attack. He can do this only once. The attack ignores non-inscribed armour and deals 3d12+6 damage, devastating a single target.
- After using Fenrir's Bane, Vidar is left vulnerable, and enemies attacking him gain advantage for the next d4 rounds as he recovers.
