# Odin, the Allfather

If Odin falls, the Nine Realms won't forget. Whatever comes next, the party will carry the weight of this battle forever.

## Running Odin
- **Overwhelming Power:** Odin doesn't hold back. Open with runic magic, summon the Einherjar to do his dirty work, and let Gungnir cut through your characters like they're nothing but leaves caught in the wind.
- **Control the Battlefield:** The Wild Hunt, storms, and collapsing terrain keep the characters scrambling. The fight should feel like they're wrestling with the weight of the world.
- **Last Phase Chaos:** When Odin goes berserk, the rules break with him. He's no longer a god at the top of the mountain—he's the mountain itself, crumbling and crushing everything in his way.

## Beating Odin
Fighting Odin isn't just combat—it's surviving a storm, outthinking a god, and standing against the full weight of fate.
- **Resourceful Use of Runes and Artefacts:** If your characters came prepared, their hard work pays off here. Runes, alliances, and artefacts shift the balance.
- **Allies and Strategy:** Characters who sought aid from gods or tricked Loki into helping have an edge. In this fight, cunning is as important as might.

## Loot and Rewards
- **Gungnir:** A weapon that strikes true, blessed and cursed in equal measure.
- **The Eye of Odin:** Odin's missing eye, which grants the wielder visions of the future. One use. The wielder can choose an effect from Loki's Severe Favour.
- **Runes of the Allfather:** The character who defeats Odin can choose one divine Rune that grants a permanent bonus (+2 to any ability of their choosing). This Rune also allows the character to cast one of Odin's Runes (from his runic abilities) once per day.

## Attacks

### Gungnir
- **Damage:** d12+4 melee/ranged
- **Effects:** Odin wields Gungnir, his spear—a weapon that rarely misses. Gungnir pierces through normal armour—only inscribed armour can reduce its damage. He can target up to three enemies with one throw. Once thrown, it always returns, like fate on a string.

### Raven's Talons
- **Damage:** d8+3
- **Effects:** Odin commands his ravens, Huginn and Muninn, to attack a character, dealing damage and giving Odin insight into that character's weaknesses. That character suffers -2 to defence for the next d4 rounds.

## Special

### Spear of Fate
- **Uses:** Once Per Battle
- **Effect:** Odin can imbue Gungnir with runes of fate, allowing him to hit every enemy within sight. Each player must Test Swift DR16 to avoid being struck.

### Fate's Unyielding Grip
- **Uses:** Once Per Battle
- **Effect:** Odin doesn't just bend fate—he grabs it by the throat. Odin may turn a player's critical success into a fumble or take back his own failure, turning it into a success. This can be done once per battle, typically at a pivotal moment.

### The Wild Hunt
- **Uses:** Once Per Battle
- **Effect:** Odin calls Sleipnir, his eight-legged horse, and charges forward like an unstoppable avalanche. Every character in his path must Test Swift DR14 or take 3d6 damage, trampled by the weight of legend itself. Those hit are knocked prone, staring up at the Allfather's shadow.

### Master of Runes
- **Uses:** One Rune Per Round
- **Effect:** Odin commands the Runes like a poet commands words, each one written with blood and power. He casts a Rune each round:
  - **Rune of Protection:** All damage Odin takes for the next round is halved.
  - **Rune of Binding:** Odin targets a character and binds them in runic chains. Test Fortitude DR14 to break free. Until freed, they are stunned.
  - **Rune of Strength:** Odin increases his damage for the next 3 rounds, adding +3 to all damage rolls.
  - **Rune of Sight:** Odin sees into the future. He automatically avoids the next successful attack against him.
  - **Rune of the Storm:** Odin calls forth a storm dealing d8 damage to all enemies he can see, forcing them to Test Fortitude DR12 or be knocked prone.
  - **Rune of the Dead:** Odin raises the spirits of fallen warriors from Valhalla as d4 Einherjar join the fight.

## Fury of the Allfather
- **Activates at:** HP < 20
- When Odin's HP dips below 20, the Allfather drops his godly composure, unleashing the beast he used to be—a raw, bloodied warrior who knows only how to destroy.
  - Odin can now make two attacks per round, either using Gungnir, Raven's Talons or both.
  - Odin is immune to all conditions except blindness.
- **The Battlefield Trembles:** As Odin enters this state, the battlefield begins to crumble. The environment becomes unstable, and at the start of each round characters must Test Swift DR14 to avoid taking d6 damage from collapsing structures, fissures, or the very sky breaking apart.
