# Gungnir

Gungnir is the mighty spear of Odin, said to have been crafted by the sons of Ivaldi. It is the symbol of Odin's authority and power, a spear that never misses its mark and is said to be imbued with runic magic, making it unbreakable and unstoppable.

- **Type:** Spear
- **Damage:** d10+5 melee/ranged
- **Special:** Gungnir rarely misses. If the wielder fumbles, they may reroll the attack once without penalty.

## Abilities

### Odin's Precision (Passive)
- Gungnir is enchanted to never miss its target. Gungnir grants +2 to all attacks. On a critical hit, the spear automatically ignores armour and deals an additional d8 damage (before doubling).

### Runic Empowerment (Once Per Session)
- Gungnir's power comes from the runic magic engraved along its shaft. Activating the runes allows the wielder to unlock its full potential. Once per session, the wielder can activate Gungnir's runes, causing it to glow with divine energy. For the next d4 rounds, the wielder gains an additional +4 to all attacks, and Gungnir's attacks deal double damage.

### Odin's Command (Once Per Day)
- Gungnir can summon the power of the Allfather to intimidate or control enemies. Once per day, the wielder can channel Odin's authority. Test Wits DR14 to compel enemies to either flee in fear or stand down for d4 rounds. The wielder can choose whether to terrify or compel submission (does not work on gods).

## Lore/Quest Hook (d6)

1. **Odin's Judgement:** Odin himself appears to the wielder, demanding Gungnir's return. The Allfather gives the wielder a choice: serve him in Ragnarok or face his divine wrath.
2. **The Sons of Ivaldi:** The Dwarves who forged Gungnir learn of its current wielder and demand a sacrifice in return for allowing them to keep the spear. Refusal could lead to a dangerous encounter with the cunning craftsmen.
3. **The Rival God:** Another Aesir, Vanir or Jotunn god learns that the wielder holds Gungnir and challenges them to a holmgang for control of the weapon. The outcome could change the balance of power between the gods.
4. **Runes of Power:** Gungnir's runes begin to reveal more power, but only if the wielder can unlock them through sacrifice or rune knowledge. A quest to learn the hidden runes could lead to great rewards—or dangerous encounters.
5. **The Betrayer:** A trusted ally becomes consumed by jealousy or fear of Gungnir's power and betrays the wielder, seeking to steal the spear for their own gain. The betrayal comes at a critical moment, and the wielder must decide whether to spare them or exact vengeance.
6. **The Eye of the Norns:** The Norns, the weavers of fate, appear to the wielder of Gungnir, offering a vision of their future. In return, they demand a piece of the wielder's soul or a life-altering decision.
