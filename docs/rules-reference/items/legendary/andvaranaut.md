# Andvaranaut (The Ring of Andvari, Bringer of Wealth & Doom)

- **Type:** Ring
- **Special:** Grants wealth but carries dangerous consequences.

## Abilities

### Wealth of Andvari (Passive)
- The wearer of Andvaranaut attracts wealth wherever they go. Once per day, the wearer can roll d100 to determine the amount of Trinkets they find, steal, or otherwise acquire. However, after three uses, Test Wits DR14. On a failure, the found Trinkets are cursed, causing bad luck (disadvantage on all Tests for the next d4 days).

### Greed's Grip (Passive)
- Andvaranaut slowly warps the mind of its wearer, turning their thoughts to greed and hoarding. Each time the wearer uses Wealth of Andvari, they gain +1 to Fortitude but suffer -1 to Wits permanently, as their mind becomes obsessed with acquiring more wealth. If Wits drops below -3, the wearer is driven to madness, obsessed with Trinkets above all else.

### Curse of Ruin (Triggered)
- The curse of Andvaranaut eventually manifests, bringing ruin to the wearer and those around them. After 7 days of wearing the ring, the GM secretly rolls d6. On a 1, the curse is triggered, and the wearer suffers a calamity—losing half their wealth and having their possessions stolen or destroyed by enemies drawn to the cursed ring.
- The curse can be lifted only by destroying Andvaranaut in the fires of Muspelheim or returning it to the Dwarves who forged it, though this will require a great quest.

## Lore/Quest Hook (d6)

1. **Andvari's Curse:** The original owner, the Dwarf Andvari, sends a vengeful spirit to reclaim his ring. The spirit stalks the wearer, causing mishaps and disasters until the ring is returned.
2. **Ruin Beckons:** A massive misfortune strikes the wearer—perhaps their gold hoard vanishes, or a powerful enemy plunders their wealth. They must seek a way to reverse this calamity, perhaps by consulting an ancient seer.
3. **Tempted by Wealth:** The wearer's growing greed draws the attention of Loki, who offers them a bargain: unimaginable riches in exchange for a favour during Ragnarok. Refusing could incur her wrath.
4. **The Ring's Rival:** Another powerful figure—perhaps a king or merchant prince—has heard of Andvaranaut's power and sends assassins to steal it. The wearer is hunted by relentless enemies who will stop at nothing to possess the ring.
5. **Breaking the Curse:** A mystic offers the wearer a chance to break Andvaranaut's curse, but only if they sacrifice something of great personal value—an ally, a magical item, or their own wealth.
6. **The Allfather's Eye:** Odin has become aware of the ring's presence and sends an emissary to test the wearer. If they fail, Odin may deem them unworthy to hold such power, cursing their fortunes even further.
