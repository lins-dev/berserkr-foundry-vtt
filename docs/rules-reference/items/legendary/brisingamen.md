# Brísingamen (The Necklace of Freyja)

- **Type:** Necklace
- **Special:** Grants significant magical and social abilities.

## Abilities

### Divine Aura (Passive)
- The wearer of Brísingamen is surrounded by a divine aura of beauty and grace. All creatures find it difficult to harm the wearer. Defence is made with advantage.

### Freyja's Blessing (Once Per Day)
- Brísingamen grants divine protection to the wearer and their allies. The wearer and all allies gain +2 to defence for d6 rounds as Brísingamen glows with divine light, shielding them from harm.

### Song of Seduction (Once Per Day)
- Brísingamen allows its wearer to manipulate the emotions of those around them, bending even the will of gods for a time. The wearer can target one creature and Test Wits DR14; on a success, the creature is charmed for d4 rounds and cannot attack or harm the wearer. On a failure, the creature becomes enraged, focusing its attacks on the wearer.

## Lore/Quest Hook (d6)

1. **Dwarven Debt:** The four Dwarves who forged Brísingamen come seeking payment, claiming Freyja never fully compensated them. They demand something of equal or greater value from the wearer or, otherwise they plan to take it by force.
2. **Divine Retribution:** Freyja herself notices the necklace is missing and sends a powerful Valkyrie to reclaim it. The Valkyrie offers the wearer a choice: return it peacefully or prepare for a deadly duel.
3. **Hearts of Stone:** The wearer's growing ability to manipulate hearts catches the attention of a jealous deity who curses them. For the next d6 days, their charm backfires, causing disadvantage on social Tests.
4. **Love and War:** The wearer draws the attention of a powerful mortal ruler who is infatuated with them due to Brísingamen's aura. The ruler's enemies, however, begin to hunt the wearer, believing them to be a threat.
5. **Gift of Freyja:** Freyja offers to bless the wearer even further, granting an additional ability for Brísingamen—but only if they complete a sacred quest in her name.
6. **Eyes of the Aesir:** The Aesir begin watching the wearer closely, suspicious of how they acquired Brísingamen. Thor or Tyr might intervene if they believe it has been stolen.
