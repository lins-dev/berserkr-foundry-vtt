# Svartalfheim: Realm of the Dwarves

Svartalfheim sits under a sky choked with ash, its mountains clawing at the heavens like pylons in a rural town. The air reeks of iron and sulphur, clinging to the skin like a curse. Waterfalls crash from jagged cliffs, roaring into the unknown, the ground constantly shifting, groaning under the weight of time. No silence here, only the hum of a restless world.

Deep in the mountains, the forges blaze. Ore veins run through the rock like blood, feeding furnaces that never die. Dwarves work in shadow and flame, hammering metal, sparks flying, the grind of their labour endless. Chains creak, powering great wheels, driven by the volcanic heart deep below—a force both life-giving and deadly.

Fimbulwinter grips the land. Tremors shake the mountains, cracks open, rockslides tear into valleys, and rivers shift course. The earth splits wide, devouring all in its path—stone, metal, flesh. But the Dwarves endure, stubborn, reinforcing their halls even as the ground threatens to swallow them.

## Weather (d6)

1. **Dust Storm**: A thick cloud of dust fills the air, reducing visibility to almost nothing. All Guile Tests are made with disadvantage. Anyone not sheltered must Test Fortitude DR12 to avoid choking on the ash, suffering d4 damage and becoming fatigued.
2. **Echoes of the Forge**: Instead of toxic fumes, the mines hum with the sound of ancient forges. The clang of hammers on anvils reverberates through the stone, a reminder of long-forgotten craftsmanship, alive with the rhythm of creation.
3. **Seismic Tremors**: A series of tremors shake the land, threatening to collapse the mountains. The party must Test Swift DR12 to avoid being trapped by falling rocks or sliding into a deep crevasse.
4. **Cleansing Breeze**: A warm, sulphur-laced gas blows through the tunnels. Though acrid, it somehow invigorates the body, as if the earth itself offers strength. All party members gain a temporary +2 to Fortitude Tests for the next hour, driven by the winds of resilience.
5. **Unstable Machinery**: The party encounters an area filled with Dwarven machinery, old and rusted, prone to malfunction. As they pass, gears begin to turn and pistons fire unpredictably. Each member must Test Swift DR12 to dodge the machinery. Fail, and suffer d6 damage from crushing or slicing gears.
6. **Lava Flow**: A sudden eruption sends a river of lava surging through the valley. The party must quickly find higher ground and Test Swift DR14 to escape the flow. Any misstep means being engulfed by the lava, dealing 2d6 fire damage. The earth burns, unforgiving and quick.

## Discoveries (d6)

1. **Dwarven Forge**: The party uncovers an ancient forge. Inside, powerful Dwarven tools and a half-completed magical weapon lie in the dust. Reactivating the forge could trigger a deadly trap or complete the construction of the weapon.
2. **Vein of Gleaming Ore**: The party stumbles upon a rich vein of rare ore, its surface gleaming with magical potential. Mining it could yield valuable materials for crafting or trade, but the act may draw the attention of nearby Dökkálfar, whose dark magic thrives on such disturbances.
3. **Crystalised Cave**: A crystal-filled cave shimmers with an eerie glow, its walls lined with precious stones. But beware—the cave is fiercely guarded by Crystal Spiders, creatures that strike at any intruder.
4. **Forgotten Vault**: The party uncovers a vault sealed by ancient runes. Inside, the riches of the Dwarven world await—runic weapons, enchanted armour, and untold treasures. But opening the vault could awaken Rock Golems, bound to protect the contents with deadly force.
5. **Dvalin's Workshop**: Hidden deep within the caves, the party discovers Dvalin's workshop, filled with cursed artefacts and unfinished creations. To handle these items safely, Test Wits DR14—for any wrong move might trigger their dark magic. But those who succeed may claim the power these twisted creations offer.
6. **Collapsed Mine**: The remains of a collapsed mine lead deeper into the earth, a maze of crumbling tunnels. These dark passages may hide lost Dwarven artefacts, but tremors rock the area, making every step a gamble.

## Rumours (d6)

1. "The vault of Andvari has been found." Whispers abound that the cursed hoard of the dwarf Andvari has been uncovered deep within the mountains. The hoard is said to contain the fabled ring Andvaranaut, which brings both fortune and ruin to any who possess it. But those who've tried to claim it never return. It's a death wish, and everyone knows it.
2. "The forges are malfunctioning." Some say the tremors have broken the forges. What was once the lifeblood of Dwarven craftsmanship has turned into a twisted, dangerous place. The fires burn uncontrolled, and even the bravest Dwarves won't venture near. A catastrophe in the making.
3. "Dvalin's curse is spreading." Rumours are swirling that Dvalin's curse is spreading. Anyone who handles his cursed creations—his twisted weapons or tools—seems to be doomed to the same fate as the old master craftsman. Slowly, they're twisted by the same corruption that turned Dvalin into the monster he became.
4. "The Dark Elves are planning something big." The Dökkálfar are gathering. No one knows exactly what they're planning, but it's bigger than anything they've done before. Dark magic is on the rise, and every time a traveller goes too deep into their territory, they don't come back.
5. "The Crystal Spiders are on the move." The Crystal Spiders have been spotted closer to the surface, moving in numbers that haven't been seen in centuries. Some believe it's a sign that something is stirring in the earth, and that these creatures are preparing for Ragnarök—though what part they'll play in it is anyone's guess.
6. "The forges are burning too hot." The Dwarven forges are burning hotter than they should be, their flames threatening to melt stone and steel alike. The heat is unbearable, and the risk of an explosion or collapse is growing by the day. If they don't get a handle on it, the whole mountain might come crashing down.

## Encounters (d8)

1. **Dokkalfar Ambush**: A group of Dark Elves, known as Dokkalfar (equal to the number of PCs) emerge from the depths, twisting reality around them, distorting the senses and creating illusions. The party must Test Wits DR14 to avoid their psychological traps. Fail, and the party finds itself disoriented, unsure of what's real.
2. **Rock Golem Awakening**: The ground trembles as d2 Rock Golems stir from their slumber, shaking off centuries of dust. The party must decide whether to fight or navigate treacherous terrain. Their strikes cause cave-ins, requiring the party to Test Swift DR12 to dodge falling rocks.
3. **Crystal Spider Nest**: The party stumbles upon a nest of d6 Crystal Spiders, their nearly invisible webs as strong as steel. They fiercely guard the crystals. Anyone attempting to take Trinkets must Test Guile DR12 to avoid being trapped. Fail, and the spiders launch a deadly ambush.
4. **The Searing Ones Lurking**: The party are followed by d4 Searing Ones, creatures of pure darkness, waiting for the right moment to attack. The party must Test Wits DR12 to sense their presence. Fail, and they manifest from their gaseous forms and attack.
5. **Fafnir's Presence**: The oppressive aura of Fafnir, the dragon who was once a Dwarf, fills the caverns. The party feels his greed and obsession echoing through the halls. Disturbing his hoard risks drawing Fafnir's deadly attention.
6. **Dvalin's Labyrinth**: The party enters Dvalin's lair, a labyrinth filled with traps and cursed artefacts. Navigating requires them to Test Wits DR12, but every wrong turn risks triggering deadly traps or unleashing one of Dvalin's twisted creations.
7. **Tremor and Rockslide**: A tremor shakes the ground, causing a rock slide. The party must Test Swift DR12 to avoid being buried or caught in the collapse. Surviving may reveal a hidden forge or chamber filled with ancient Dwarven tools and relics.
8. **Dokkalfar Conclave**: The party stumbles upon a secret Dokkalfar meeting, where the Dark Elves plot something sinister. Test Guile DR12 to spy on the gathering to glean valuable information.

## Creatures of Svartalfheim

### Dokkalfar (Dark Elves)
- **HP:** 5
- **Armour:** -
- **Weapon:** Shadow blast d8 ranged
- **Special:** Mind Maze. The Dökkálfar can warp a target's perception, making them question their surroundings. The target must Test Wits DR12 or be mentally ensnared, suffering -1 to all Tests as they struggle to focus on reality.

### Rock Golems
- **HP:** 17
- **Armour:** Stone and metal skin - d3
- **Weapon:** Crushing slam d8
- **Special:** Stone Sentinel. Rock Golems are indistinguishable from natural rock until they attack. Any creature attempting to perceive them before they move must Test Wits DR14 or assume the golem is just part of the landscape.

### The Searing Ones
- **HP:** 8
- **Armour:** -
- **Weapon:** Claw d4, Bite d6
- **Special:** Sulphur Shift. The Searing Ones can move between mists and gasses, vanishing and reappearing in the blink of an eye.

### Crystal Spiders
- **HP:** 2
- **Armour:** Crystal thorax - d4
- **Weapon:** Infectious bite d4
- **Special:** Glittering Lure. Crystal Spiders are drawn to those carrying the most Trinkets. They will always attack the character with the highest Trinket count first. Their poisonous bite requires you to Test Fortitude DR10; failure results in being Poisoned.

### Dvalin
- **HP:** 20
- **Armour:** Masterwork armour - d3
- **Weapon:** Enchanted hammer d10+2 (deals an extra d4 damage if the target's armour has a Rune inscribed)
- **Special:** Trapmaster. The first time a character steps into Dvalin's lair, they must Test Swift DR14 to avoid one of his cursed traps. Failing the Test results in d6 damage and a temporary -1 to Guile and Might Tests for as long as they remain in his domain. Goon Summon. Once per battle, Dvalin can summon two loyal Dwarven followers (8HP, Armour - d2, Weapon d6) to aid him if the battle turns against him.
- **Reward:** Dvalin's Armring. A one-time activation can give the user +3 to all Wits Tests for the rest of the day. (Cursed. After activation, the user will be hunted by Heimdall).

### Fafnir the Dragon
- **HP:** 37
- **Armour:** Dragon scales - d2
- **Weapon:** Fire breath d10+3, Claws d6
- **Special:** Sulphuric Breath. Fafnir's breath attack melts d100 Trinkets instantly. Additionally, anyone caught in the blast must Test Fortitude DR14 or have their armour degraded by 2 tiers. Treasure Madness. Fafnir's presence amplifies greed in others. Every character must Test Wits DR12 each round they spend within Fafnir's lair. Failing the Test means they become fixated on his hoard, suffering a -1 to attacks against Fafnir as they eye the treasure obsessively.
- **Reward:** Fafnir's Searing Armour. Tier 4 Armour that imposes no increased DR to Swift Tests or Defence but cannot be enchanted.
